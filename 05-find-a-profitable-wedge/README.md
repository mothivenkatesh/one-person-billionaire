# Lesson 05: Find a Profitable Wedge

## TL;DR

The single biggest reason indie AI products fail isn't bad code — it's bad targeting. "AI assistant for everyone" loses to "AI lien-search assistant for Florida real estate paralegals" every time. Pick the narrowest specific painful workflow you can find a paying audience for. The math is simpler than you think.

## Core idea

```
A profitable wedge has 3 attributes:

1. PAIN          A real workflow people swear at, weekly
2. PAYER         Someone with budget authority and a credit card
3. REACHABLE     A channel where you can find 1,000 of them
```

Miss any one and the business dies.

## How it works in practice

### The micro-niche math

```
1,000 customers  ×  $100 / month  =  $1.2M ARR
  100 customers  ×  $1,000/month  =  $1.2M ARR
   10 customers  ×  $10K/month   =  $1.2M ARR
```

You don't need to "win the market." You need 1,000 customers who pay $100, OR 100 who pay $1K. Both are achievable. The "win the market" framing kills more startups than competition.

### Where to find pain

Watch where humans complain *with specificity*:

| Source | What you're looking for |
|---|---|
| **Reddit** (industry subs) | Recurring "how do I…" + "this software sucks at…" |
| **G2 reviews** | 1-3 star reviews with specific gripes (not "bad UX") |
| **Twitter/X** | Niche community accounts venting about workflow pain |
| **Forums** (legal, accounting, real estate, healthcare) | Where pros help each other — pain shows up as repeated questions |
| **Job postings** | If 200 firms are hiring "data entry specialist for X," X is automatable |
| **Your own past job** | Workflows you hated. Probably automatable now. |
| **Customer support transcripts** (if you have them) | Where do people give up? |

Bad source: HackerNews. Self-selecting for engineers who build things themselves.

### The 3 attributes — scored

For each candidate wedge, score 1–5:

| Attribute | 1 (bad) | 5 (great) |
|---|---|---|
| **Pain frequency** | Once a year | Multiple times a week |
| **Pain intensity** | Mild irritation | "I'd pay $500 to never do this again" |
| **Payer access** | Person feeling pain has no budget | Person feeling pain has a corporate card |
| **Channel reachability** | Scattered across the internet | Concentrated in 1–3 communities |
| **Manual workaround cost** | $5/hour offshore VA | $80/hour licensed professional |
| **Workflow stability** | Changes every 6 months | Stable for 5+ years |
| **Defensibility** | Anyone can copy in a weekend | You need domain knowledge / data / trust |

Add it up. Anything <25 is probably not worth your 5 years. Aim for 30+.

### Anti-wedges (why "X for Y" usually fails)

- **"Notion for lawyers"** — lawyers already use Notion or LexisNexis; switching cost > benefit
- **"AI Slack assistant"** — Slack has its own; you can't out-Slack Slack
- **"GPT wrapper for therapists"** — regulated, low budget, retention nightmare
- **"AI for content creators"** — too many competitors, low ARPU, zero retention
- **"Anything for engineers"** — engineers build their own and don't pay

### Wedge archetypes that work

| Archetype | Why |
|---|---|
| **Compliance / regulatory automation** | High pain, high stakes, willing payers |
| **Workflow automation in a regulated industry** | Healthcare, legal, financial, insurance |
| **B2B sales tooling for niche verticals** | Sales teams have budget, visible ROI |
| **Replacing a $50/hr offshore role with a $99/mo agent** | Clean ROI math |
| **Industry-specific "second brain" for solo professionals** | Real estate agents, financial advisors, doctors |
| **Migration / integration agents** | Stripe → Adyen, Salesforce → HubSpot, Shopify → custom |

## Common traps

| Trap | Why |
|---|---|
| Building for "everyone" | You'll reach no one |
| Building for engineers | They build their own |
| Building for free users hoping to convert later | Free users don't convert |
| Picking a wedge with no payer | Pain ≠ revenue |
| Picking a wedge you can't reach | "TAM is huge" doesn't matter if you can't reach the first 100 |
| Choosing a wedge in a hot vibe-coded space (general "AI assistants", coding copilots) | You're competing with $100M-funded companies |

## The "5 wedges" exercise

Brainstorm **5 candidate wedges** today. For each, fill in:

```
WEDGE NAME:
PAIN (specific workflow):
WHO FEELS IT (job title + industry):
HOW OFTEN (per week):
WHAT THEY DO TODAY (current workaround):
WHAT THEY'D PAY (per month):
WHERE TO REACH 100 OF THEM (specific community):
```

Then score each on the table above. Pick the **highest-scoring one** and run it through Lesson 06's Riskiest Assumption Test.

## Exercise

Spend the next **48 hours** doing only one thing:
1. Pick **3 industry subreddits** (real estate, healthcare admin, accounting, etc.)
2. Read the top 100 posts of the past 6 months
3. List every recurring complaint about software / workflow / manual work
4. Cluster the complaints
5. Pick the cluster you find both valuable AND not boring

That cluster is your candidate wedge. Don't open a code editor until you've done this.
