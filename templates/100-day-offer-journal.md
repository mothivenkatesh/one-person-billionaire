# 100-Day Offer Journal

*Adapted from Hormozi's $100M Journal pattern. One prompt per day for 100 days. Each prompt takes 10 minutes. By day 100 you'll have iterated your offer ~10× and have the operator's pattern recognition.*

How to use:
- One prompt per day
- 10 minutes max
- Write in a notebook, Notion, or here
- Don't skip days — the compound is the whole point
- At day 30, 60, 100 — review and rebuild your offer canvas

---

## Days 1-10: Crowd diagnosis

**Day 1:** List the 3 most painful workflows your customer experiences in a typical week. For each, rate the intensity 1-10.
**Day 2:** Of those 3, which has the highest combined score on (a) pain × (b) frequency × (c) purchasing power? That's your primary wedge.
**Day 3:** Where does this customer hang out online? List 5 specific places. Rank by reachability.
**Day 4:** Talk to one person in the wedge. What did they say? Write it verbatim.
**Day 5:** What's the customer doing today instead of using your product? What does the workaround cost them?
**Day 6:** Score your wedge: pain (1-10) + power (1-10) + concentration (1-10) + growth (1-10) = __/40.
**Day 7:** Re-write your dream outcome statement in the customer's voice (use words from Day 4).
**Day 8:** What identity does the customer aspire to? (E.g., "operator who has it together.")
**Day 9:** Talk to a *churned* customer (or one who said no). Why didn't they buy/stay?
**Day 10:** Crowd assessment: is this a starving crowd? Yes / No / Unsure → write 3 sentences.

---

## Days 11-20: Problem inventory

**Day 11:** List 10 problems between your customer and the dream outcome. Be specific.
**Day 12:** For each problem, rate it: how often (daily/weekly/quarterly) and how painful (1-10).
**Day 13:** Which 3 problems show up in every customer conversation? Those are your offer's center of gravity.
**Day 14:** What do customers blame for these problems? (Themselves? Tools? Other people? Time?) Misattribution is data.
**Day 15:** What's the cost (in $$, hours, opportunity) of NOT solving each top-3 problem?
**Day 16:** Talk to one new prospect. Did your top-3 list match their reality? Update.
**Day 17:** Map each top-3 problem to a solution your product provides. Any gaps?
**Day 18:** For any gap, decide: build the solution OR drop the problem from the offer.
**Day 19:** Rewrite your offer pitch using only top-3 problems → solutions language. No features.
**Day 20:** Test the new pitch on 1 prospect. What was their reaction?

---

## Days 21-30: Value Equation tuning

**Day 21:** Score your offer 1-10 on each of: Dream Outcome, Likelihood, Time, Effort. Lowest lever?
**Day 22:** Add ONE testimonial (named, specific, with numbers) to your pricing page. ↑ Likelihood.
**Day 23:** Reduce time-to-first-result by ONE step. What can be pre-loaded / done-for-you / instant?
**Day 24:** Reduce customer effort by ONE step. What can you remove from setup?
**Day 25:** Re-score Value Equation. Did your scores move? By how much?
**Day 26:** Revisit your dream outcome — is it specific enough? Time-bound? Emotional?
**Day 27:** What's the most common objection in sales calls? Write it verbatim.
**Day 28:** Brainstorm 3 ways to kill that objection in the offer (bonus, guarantee, copy).
**Day 29:** Implement the best one this week.
**Day 30:** **MILESTONE — rebuild your Grand Slam offer canvas from scratch.** Compare to day 1.

---

## Days 31-40: Bonus stack

**Day 31:** List every objection you've heard from prospects/customers in the last 30 days.
**Day 32:** Cluster objections into 5-7 groups. The top 3-5 groups are your bonus targets.
**Day 33:** For each top objection, design a bonus that kills it. Name it. Give it a stated value.
**Day 34:** Are your bonuses each 5-10× more valuable than the *time/effort* needed to redeem them? If not, the customer won't bother.
**Day 35:** Total bonus value should be 5-10× the offer price. Calculate. Adjust.
**Day 36:** Add the bonus stack to your pitch. Test on 1 prospect.
**Day 37:** Did the bonus stack make the offer feel "stupid to say no"? If not, what's missing?
**Day 38:** Drop your weakest bonus. Add a stronger one.
**Day 39:** Test the revised stack on 1 prospect. Compare reaction.
**Day 40:** **CHECK** — your bonus stack should now be a clear lever, not noise.

---

## Days 41-50: Guarantees

**Day 41:** Read the [4 guarantee types](../skills/grand-slam-offer/references/guarantee-types.md). Which fits your product?
**Day 42:** Draft a Conditional + Outcome guarantee for your offer. Be specific about conditions and outcome.
**Day 43:** Stress-test: what's the worst customer that could exploit this guarantee? Add a condition that filters them.
**Day 44:** Stress-test the other way: would a *good-fit* customer feel the conditions are unreasonable? Adjust.
**Day 45:** Add the guarantee prominently to your pitch and pricing page.
**Day 46:** Test on 1 prospect. Did the guarantee accelerate their decision?
**Day 47:** Did your refund rate change? If too high → tighten conditions. If zero → loosen them.
**Day 48:** Calculate: (close rate lift × ARPU) − (refund rate × cost-to-serve). Net positive?
**Day 49:** Refine the guarantee language. Show it to a non-customer for clarity check.
**Day 50:** **MILESTONE — re-score Value Equation. Likelihood should be ≥ 8/10.**

---

## Days 51-60: Pricing

**Day 51:** What's your current price? When was the last time you raised it?
**Day 52:** Quote your next prospect 1.5× your current price. Track close rate.
**Day 53:** Quote your next 3 prospects 2× your current price. Track close rate.
**Day 54:** Did revenue per close go up at the new price? Even with lower close rate?
**Day 55:** Identify your "low-friction" tier and "high-value" tier. Are they different products or just different prices?
**Day 56:** Add an annual plan with 15-20% discount. Push it in sales.
**Day 57:** Talk to a customer who paid annual. Why did they pick annual?
**Day 58:** Calculate LTV difference between monthly and annual customers.
**Day 59:** Move 1 monthly customer to annual via outbound offer. What worked?
**Day 60:** **CHECK** — your pricing should now make ~20% of prospects say "expensive."

---

## Days 61-70: Naming + positioning

**Day 61:** What does your product currently call itself? Is it generic? "AI [thing]"? Rename.
**Day 62:** Use the MAGIC formula: [Magnetic Why] + [Avatar] + [Grand Outcome] + [Interval] + [Container]. Generate 10 candidates.
**Day 63:** Score each candidate on Avatar Specificity (1-10), Outcome Clarity (1-10), Memorability (1-10).
**Day 64:** Pick the top 3. Test on 5 people. Which did they remember 24 hours later?
**Day 65:** Update your homepage, pricing page, and sales pitch with the new name.
**Day 66:** Update your X bio, LinkedIn header, email signature.
**Day 67:** Did inbound mention the new name? Track 7 days of inbound.
**Day 68:** Refine the headline below the name — what does the avatar get in 1 sentence?
**Day 69:** A/B test old name vs new name on the same outbound list. Track reply rate.
**Day 70:** **MILESTONE — your name should now answer: who's it for? what do they get? in what time?**

---

## Days 71-80: Proof / Likelihood

**Day 71:** Count your testimonials. How many are named, specific, with numbers?
**Day 72:** Email 3 happy customers asking for a testimonial with metric ("X result in Y time").
**Day 73:** Record a 60-second case study video with one customer (Loom is fine).
**Day 74:** Add the new testimonials to your pricing page.
**Day 75:** Build one before/after asset: screenshot or workflow diagram.
**Day 76:** Add it to your homepage above the fold.
**Day 77:** What's a controversial industry claim you can make + back with data? Write it.
**Day 78:** Publish that claim somewhere (X thread, LinkedIn post, blog).
**Day 79:** Track inbound mentioning the claim/asset. What's the conversion rate?
**Day 80:** **CHECK** — Likelihood lever should now be visibly higher.

---

## Days 81-90: Distribution × Offer alignment

**Day 81:** Open Lesson 09 (Build in Public). Is your daily/weekly post talking about the offer's outcome — or your features?
**Day 82:** Rewrite your last 5 posts to lead with the customer's dream outcome.
**Day 83:** Open Lesson 10 (Cold Outbound). Does your cold message use the new name + outcome?
**Day 84:** Rewrite your cold message template using the offer language.
**Day 85:** Send 25 messages with the new template. Track reply rate.
**Day 86:** Compare to your old reply rate. Did the offer-aligned message convert better?
**Day 87:** Open Lesson 11 (Content). Pick 1 compounding asset that aligns with the offer (calculator, vs page, data report).
**Day 88:** Build it.
**Day 89:** Ship it. Add email capture. Tie it to the offer.
**Day 90:** **MILESTONE — your distribution and offer should now reinforce each other.**

---

## Days 91-100: Compound

**Day 91:** Re-score your Value Equation. Compare to day 25 score. How much did it move?
**Day 92:** Re-do the Crowd score. Has the wedge gotten clearer? Or do you need to narrow further?
**Day 93:** Talk to 3 customers. Ask: "Why did you buy?" Capture their words.
**Day 94:** Talk to 3 churned customers. Ask: "Why did you leave?" Capture their words.
**Day 95:** Cross-reference: do the buy reasons match your dream outcome? Do the churn reasons map to a missing bonus or weak guarantee?
**Day 96:** Pick the ONE biggest gap from days 93-95. Plan a 2-week sprint to fix it.
**Day 97:** Calculate your year-1 trajectory based on current MRR + growth rate. Realistic?
**Day 98:** Read Lesson 20 (The 10-Year Compound). Re-write your 10-year statement.
**Day 99:** Pick the 3 things from this 100-day journal that moved the needle most.
**Day 100:** **REBUILD** the Grand Slam offer canvas one more time. Compare to day 1, day 30, day 60. **The diff is your operator's pattern recognition. That's the asset.**

---

## After day 100

Restart the cycle. The offer is never done. Hormozi's data: top operators iterate offers ~50 times before they hit the version that converts at 30%+.

You're now in the 1% of operators who actually iterate the offer instead of the product.
