# PM Skills That Compound vs Plateau

## Purpose

Some PM skills make a 10-year PM 10x better than a 5-year PM. Others top out in 18 months and never improve. The distinction matters because most PMs invest in plateau skills (frameworks, tools, certifications) and wonder why their career doesn't compound. This skill names which is which, so you can invest in the compounding side and stop over-investing in the plateau side.

This skill applies the framework from Institute of Product Leadership's "PM Skills That Compound vs Plateau."

## When to Use

- Planning your skill investment for the next year
- Choosing between two roles (one plateau-heavy, one compounding-heavy)
- Mentoring a junior PM who keeps asking "should I learn X?"
- Deciding what to deprioritize when overloaded
- Considering a certification or course that promises career growth

## How to Tell Them Apart

**Compounding skills:**
- Built through accumulated experience with real product decisions
- Create "pattern libraries" that improve judgement over time
- Cannot be fully taught in a course or replicated by an AI tool
- Show non-linear growth — 10-year PM is meaningfully better than 5-year PM
- Require feedback on outcomes (shipped product, real users) to develop

**Plateau skills:**
- Learnable in weeks to a few months
- Show rapid mastery then minimal marginal return
- Increasingly automatable by AI
- Reset when tools/frameworks change
- Provide diminishing career differentiation over time

## The Five Compounding Skills (Invest Here)

### 1. Problem Judgement
Distinguishing problems that truly merit solving from problems that are visible / political / trendy. Develops by sitting with the consequences of past calls.

### 2. Knowing What NOT to Build
Confidence to cut scope grows from track record. New PMs add features to feel useful; senior PMs cut features to ship. The cuts reveal judgement.

### 3. Pattern Recognition for Failure Modes
Spotting "I've seen this fail before" before it materializes. Built from witnessing 10+ failures, ideally a few you owned.

### 4. User Instinct
Weighing stated vs. behavioral signals. Knowing when survey data lies. Reading silence. Built by direct, sustained user contact over years.

### 5. Strategic Communication
Adaptive influence — changing minds, not just delivering information clearly. Includes framing, sequencing, and reading the room. The hardest to teach.

## The Five Plateau Skills (Don't Over-Invest)

### 1. Technical Fluency
APIs, architecture diagrams, database tradeoffs. Learnable in months. Useful, but stops differentiating after the bar is met.

### 2. Stakeholder Management Processes
Update cadences, doc templates, alignment rituals. Mastered in ~18 months. Beyond that, returns are tiny.

### 3. Tool Proficiency
Jira, Figma, Amplitude, Linear. Weeks to learn. Constantly deprecated and replaced. Don't list these on your CV as a competitive advantage.

### 4. PRD / Spec Writing
The structure is known. AI handles first drafts better than most PMs. Editing skill matters more than writing skill — and editing is a sub-skill of Strategic Communication.

### 5. Process Frameworks
Agile, OKRs, Shape Up. Useful baseline; narrow ROI on deep investment in any single one. Don't become an "OKRs PM."

## Career Investment Strategy

**Prioritize:**
- Proximity to real product outcomes and accountability
- Honest reflection on the consequences of decisions you owned
- Sustained user engagement (1:1s, support shifts, sales calls)
- Sitting in difficult situations long enough for data to land
- Protecting cognitive wrestling with ambiguity — resist AI shortcuts for the *thinking* parts

**Deprioritize:**
- Certifications and tool-proficiency collection
- Process optimization beyond competence
- Framework specialization beyond literacy
- Roles that maximize plateau skills (heavy process, light decision-making)

## Process

1. **Audit your last 12 months of skill investment.** Hours spent learning X — sort by compounding vs. plateau.
2. **Calculate the ratio.** Most PMs are 70/30 plateau-favored. The senior ones flip it.
3. **Identify the one compounding skill you're weakest at.** Pick one — not five.
4. **Design a 12-week investment.** What role choice, project, mentor, or habit will move that skill?
5. **Cut the heaviest plateau investment.** Reclaim that time for the compounding work.

## Output

A career investment plan with:
- Current compounding/plateau split (from audit)
- Top compounding skill to invest in this year
- One concrete project / habit / role move to develop it
- One plateau investment you're cutting

## Tips

- "I learned OKRs deeply" is not a brag at senior PM level. "I judged 5 hard product calls and recorded which were right" is.
- AI is taking the plateau skills first. Investing there is a depreciating asset.
- The compounding skills require failure feedback. If you've never owned a decision that went badly, you don't have them yet — find a role where you can.
