# Product Vision Board (Roman Pichler)

## Purpose

The Product Vision Board is a one-page strategic artifact that forces clarity on five things a product must answer: why it exists, who it serves, what problem it solves, what it ships, and why the business invests. It sits between vision (too abstract) and PRD (too detailed) — and aligns teams faster than a 20-slide deck.

This skill applies Roman Pichler's framework.

## When to Use

- Defining a new product or major feature line
- Repositioning an existing product after a pivot
- Onboarding new team members to the product strategy
- Aligning stakeholders on a single artifact before writing a roadmap
- Replacing a vague vision deck with something testable

## The Five Sections

### 1. Vision
**Question:** What positive change does this product create in the world?

**Rules:**
- Big and inspiring; can be a slogan or one sentence
- Motivational AND achievable — both, not either
- Guides every prioritization decision downstream
- Acts as the north star when tradeoffs get hard

**Example:** "Help every Indian D2C brand collect payments without hand-coding integrations."

### 2. Target Group
**Question:** Who specifically does this product serve?

**Rules:**
- Distinguish **users** (interact with the product) from **customers** (decide / pay)
- Pick homogenous, well-defined segments — not "SMBs in general"
- B2B: list both user personas AND buyer personas separately
- Prioritize users over internal stakeholders

**Example:** "Users: Shopify-store founders running ₹50L-5Cr GMV. Customers: same person."

### 3. Needs
**Question:** What problem does this solve, from the user's perspective?

**Rules:**
- Frame as user problems, NOT solution features
- Articulate what success looks like for users and customers
- Convey why people will use or pay
- Prioritize if there are multiple needs

**Example:** "Founders lose 6-8 hrs/month reconciling 4 payment-method reports manually."

### 4. Product
**Question:** What 3-5 features deliver the value?

**Rules:**
- Hard cap: **5 features maximum**
- Each feature must directly satisfy a stated need
- Focus on differentiation (USP), not feature parity
- Detailed specs belong in the backlog — NOT here

**Example:** "Unified settlement report · auto-categorization · CSV export to Tally · refund traceability · search."

### 5. Business Goals
**Question:** Why should the company invest?

**Rules:**
- Be specific about the business benefit (revenue, market entry, cost reduction, learning, brand)
- Set measurable targets where possible
- Prioritize when multiple goals exist
- Internal products still need this section

**Example:** "Increase merchant retention from 78% to 85% in 12 months · ARPU lift ₹400/merchant/quarter."

## Extended Board (Optional)

For new products or major pivots, add:
- Competitors
- Partners
- Channels
- Revenue streams
- Cost factors

Use only when business-model uncertainty is high.

## Process

1. **Draft alone.** Fill all five sections in 30-45 minutes. Don't workshop the v1.
2. **Stress-test the Vision → Needs link.** Does the vision logically connect to the needs you listed?
3. **Stress-test the Needs → Product link.** Does each feature directly solve a need? Cut features that don't.
4. **Workshop v2 with the team.** Capture disagreements. Disagreement = clarity work needed.
5. **Identify the biggest assumption.** Run a problem interview or observation to validate it.
6. **Update and re-share.** The board is a living document; expect 3-5 revisions in the first quarter.

## Output

A one-page Vision Board (Notion / Miro / PDF) with all 5 sections filled, plus:
- The biggest assumption flagged with a planned validation method
- Owner + last-updated date
- Linked from the PRD, roadmap, and onboarding doc

## Tips

- A great vision fits on a sticky note. If it needs a paragraph, it's not a vision.
- Five features is a hard cap. The 6th feature is hiding the lack of focus elsewhere.
- The board is a thinking tool, not a deliverable. Re-do it when learning lands.
- If two team members fill the board independently and produce different boards, you haven't aligned — you've documented confusion.
