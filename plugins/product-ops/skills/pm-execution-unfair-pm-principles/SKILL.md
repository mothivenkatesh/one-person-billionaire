# Unfair PM Role: Operating Principles

## Purpose

The PM role is structurally unfair — you're a maker and a manager simultaneously, you have responsibility without hiring authority, and org-flattening keeps adding load without resources. Fairness isn't coming. The principles below redistribute cognitive load so you can do the actual product work.

This skill applies the framework from Lenny's "Product manager is an unfair role. So work unfairly."

## When to Use

- You're consistently working past 7pm and still behind
- A flat org just dropped 2 more surface areas on you
- A new PM on the team is drowning
- Your calendar is 70%+ meetings and your output is slipping
- You realize Slack is your default workflow and that's the problem

## Why the Role Is Unfair

- PMs run on maker AND manager schedules simultaneously
- No hiring/firing authority but full outcome responsibility
- Org buffer for absorbed dysfunction
- Flattening = more IC burden, same headcount
- External stakeholders default to you for decisions you don't own

## Seven Operating Principles

### 1. Eliminate Task Accumulation Live
Complete action items during the meeting on screenshare. No "I'll send the doc later." The doc is being typed while you talk.

**Practice:** Open the Notion / doc on screen. Update it live as decisions land. Send the link before the meeting ends.

### 2. Replace Meetings With Async (under 60-second Loom)
For nuanced communication, record a Loom under 60 seconds. Aim for 2 takes max — the third take is fake-polish.

**Practice:** If you're about to schedule a 30-min "quick sync to align," record a 45-sec Loom instead. Schedule the meeting only if Loom doesn't resolve it.

### 3. Boundary-Set on Slack
Don't open Slack for the first 4 hours of your day. Use sections to hide noisy channels. Set reminders for follow-ups so you can close threads without holding them in your head.

**Practice:**
- 9am-1pm: Slack off. Do the real work.
- Sections: collapse all channels you don't own.
- Mute first, unmute later if you missed something important.

### 4. Build Self-Reliant Teams
When someone DMs you with a decision they should make, redirect to the expert. Celebrate out-of-role initiative publicly. Avoid being the bottleneck.

**Practice:** Reply with "I think [Expert] is the right person — looping them in" instead of giving the answer yourself.

### 5. Pre-Gather Insights (Scrapbook)
Maintain a lightweight Notion database organized by strategy area. Drop every interesting customer quote, competitor screenshot, internal anecdote, metric anomaly into it as you encounter them.

**Practice:** A 2-minute weekly habit yields dividends during planning cycles. By Q-end you have 30 pieces of evidence ready.

### 6. Delegate Writing (where you can)
PRDs, recaps, release notes — anything where the structure is known, drafted by AI or a colleague, reviewed by you.

**Practice:** Start with AI-drafted recaps from meeting transcripts. Edit, don't write from scratch.

### 7. Redefine Success Metrics
Stop measuring yourself on meeting attendance, Slack response time, or doc volume. Measure on outcomes shipped and learning velocity.

**Practice:** Each Friday, write "what shipped, what we learned, what we changed our minds about." That's the only weekly self-review that matters.

## Process

1. **Audit one week.** Where does your time go? Meetings, Slack, deep work — bucket it.
2. **Identify the worst-leaking bucket.** Usually Slack or meetings.
3. **Apply the matching principle for 2 weeks.** Don't try all 7 at once.
4. **Measure delta.** Hours back, output shipped, energy at end of week.
5. **Add the next principle once the first is habitual.**

## Output

A personal operating system with:
- A defined Slack window (start time + duration)
- A meeting-default-to-Loom policy with a written threshold
- A scrapbook started and being filled weekly
- A Friday outcomes review replacing whatever self-evaluation you do now

## Tips

- Trying to install all 7 principles at once fails. Pick one, install, then next.
- "Fairness isn't coming" is the meta-principle. Stop waiting for the org to fix it.
- Manager-mode and maker-mode have to be physically separated in your week, not interleaved.
- The scrapbook is the single highest-ROI habit on this list.
