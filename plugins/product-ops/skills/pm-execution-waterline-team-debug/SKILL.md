# Waterline Model: Debugging Teams That Aren't Working

## Purpose

When a team underperforms, the default is to blame individuals. The Waterline Model says: don't. Work from the outside in — Structure, then Dynamics, then Interpersonal, then Individual. Most team dysfunction is rational adaptation to a broken system; fixing the system unblocks the people.

This skill applies the framework from Lenny's "How to debug a team that isn't working: the Waterline Model."

## When to Use

- The team keeps missing goals despite working hard
- Decisions get reversed days after being made
- Two people on the team have visible friction
- You're about to performance-manage someone out and want to be sure
- A new manager inherits an underperforming team

## The Four Layers (Top → Bottom)

### 1. Structure (Above waterline)
**Covers:** Vision, goals, roles, ownership, success criteria, org design.

**Symptoms of breakage:**
- Team members can't articulate their goals or owned metrics
- "Whose decision is this?" comes up weekly
- Definitions of success differ across leadership

**Diagnose with:**
- Can each person state their role, goals, and KPIs in one sentence?
- Are success definitions consistent up the chain?

**Intervene by:** Clarifying mandate, redefining roles, re-aligning goals. Most apparent "people problems" resolve here.

### 2. Dynamics (Above waterline)
**Covers:** Decision-making norms, conflict resolution, info flow, cultural defaults.

**Symptoms:**
- Decisions get reversed after they're made
- Bottlenecks despite clear role assignments
- Teams optimizing for safety over progress

**Diagnose with:**
- Are decisions stable once made?
- Do people feel safe moving quickly?
- What does leadership reinforce by their own behavior?

**Intervene by:** Changing interaction rules and demanding leadership consistency. Hard, because it requires leaders to examine their own patterns.

### 3. Interpersonal (Below waterline)
**Covers:** Trust between specific individuals, style friction, history.

**Symptoms:**
- Two leaders visibly distrust each other
- Information hoarding between teams
- Decisions slow because of relationship tension

**Critical check:** Often what looks interpersonal is actually structural overlap leaking down. **Rule out Structure and Dynamics first.**

**Intervene by:** Naming the tension explicitly, anchoring it in business impact, making relationship quality an expectation. May require direct repair work or a personnel change.

### 4. Individual (Below waterline)
**Covers:** Skill gaps, stress, confidence, personal circumstances.

**Symptoms:**
- Performance gaps persist after Structure/Dynamics/Interpersonal are sound
- Specific role requirements can't be met

**Critical check:** Only valid when the layers above have been ruled out. Otherwise you're firing the symptom.

**Intervene by:** Evaluating the person against the role honestly. Decide on coaching investment vs. role change. Avoid prolonged ambiguity.

## Diagnostic Sequence

```
START
  ↓
Structure   ← most common culprit. Fix here first.
  ↓
Dynamics    ← if Structure is sound
  ↓
Interpersonal  ← only after ruling out systems
  ↓
Individual  ← last resort, only when system is proven sound
```

## Process

1. **Snorkel first.** Write down what you see — symptoms, not causes.
2. **Walk the layers top-down.** For each layer, ask the diagnostic questions. Record the layer where the first real breakage appears.
3. **Intervene at the highest broken layer.** Don't skip down to "this person is the problem."
4. **Wait 2-4 weeks.** Systems take time to respond. Don't re-diagnose during the noise.
5. **Re-evaluate.** If symptoms persist, walk down one layer.

## Output

A team-diagnosis writeup with:
- The symptoms observed
- The layer identified as broken
- The intervention plan
- The success criteria to revisit in 2-4 weeks

## Tips

- "This person is the problem" is almost always either a Structure or Dynamics problem in disguise.
- If you're a new manager: assume Structure is broken until proven otherwise.
- Two layers can be broken at once — fix the higher one first.
- Reorgs are blunt instruments. Use them when Dynamics is so broken that Structure interventions can't land.
