# Product Strategy Canvas (Roman Pichler)

## Purpose

Lean Canvas is great for startups validating a business model. Business Model Canvas is great for finance and ops. Neither is quite right for working PMs defining product strategy mid-flight. Pichler's Product Strategy Canvas fills the gap — a one-page artifact that captures Need, Target Group, Business Goals, Stand Out, and Key Features in language tuned for product teams.

This skill applies Roman Pichler's Product Strategy Canvas framework.

## When to Use

- Defining product strategy for an existing product going into a new chapter
- Sharpening positioning when the team is losing the "why us" thread
- Onboarding new PMs or eng leads to the strategic context
- Replacing a 30-slide strategy deck with a single page
- Auditing whether the team can articulate strategy consistently

## The Five Sections

### 1. Need (Problem Solved)
The primary user problem the product addresses. Stated from the user's perspective, not the solution's.

**Question:** What user problem does this product solve that's worth solving?

**Example:** "Indian D2C founders lose 6-8 hrs/month reconciling payment-method reports manually."

### 2. Target Group (Market)
Specific user and customer segments. Distinguish users (who interact) from customers (who pay/decide).

**Question:** Who exactly experiences this problem and is willing to act on solving it?

**Example:** "Shopify-store founders running ₹50L-5Cr GMV; same person is user and customer."

### 3. Business Goals (Strategic Value)
Why the company invests in solving this problem. Revenue, market entry, defensibility, learning, brand. Set targets where possible.

**Question:** What strategic value does this product create for the business?

**Example:** "Lock in D2C merchant retention at 85% (up from 78%) and add ₹400/quarter ARPU lift."

### 4. Stand Out (Differentiation)
What makes this product distinct. Not feature parity — the genuine reason to choose it over alternatives.

**Question:** Why would a target user choose this over what they use today (or build themselves)?

**Example:** "Only solution that auto-categorizes payments across 4 methods AND exports to Tally with zero merchant config."

### 5. Key Features (Product)
Top 3-5 features that deliver the differentiation. NOT a feature list — the features that matter strategically.

**Question:** What 3-5 features make the differentiation real?

**Example:** "Unified settlement report · Auto-categorization engine · Tally CSV export · Refund traceability · Cross-method search."

## How It Differs From Other Canvases

| Canvas | Focus | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Lean Canvas** | Validating an unproven business model | Pre-PMF startups |
| **Business Model Canvas** | Commercial mechanics (revenue, costs, partners) | Business-side strategy |
| **Pichler Strategy Canvas** | Product strategy in user/problem/differentiation language | Working PMs running existing products |
| **Product Vision Board** (also Pichler) | Higher-level — vision + target + needs + product + business goals | Earlier-stage; before strategy canvas |

The Vision Board and Strategy Canvas are complementary — Vision Board sets direction, Strategy Canvas operationalizes it for a specific product chapter.

## Process

1. **Draft alone in 30-45 minutes.** Don't workshop the v1.
2. **Stress-test the Need → Stand Out link.** If your differentiation doesn't directly address the stated need, the strategy is incoherent.
3. **Stress-test the Stand Out → Features link.** If your top features don't make the differentiation real, you're listing features for habit, not strategy.
4. **Workshop with the team.** Where the team disagrees = where strategy needs clarification.
5. **Identify the biggest assumption.** Most often it's in "Stand Out" — is the differentiation real, or aspirational?
6. **Validate the assumption.** Customer interview, competitive teardown, or behavioral data.
7. **Update quarterly.** The canvas is a live artifact, not a one-time deliverable.

## Output

A one-page Strategy Canvas (Notion / Miro / PDF) with:
- All 5 sections filled
- The biggest unvalidated assumption flagged
- A planned validation method for that assumption
- Owner + last-updated date
- Linked from the PRD, roadmap, and onboarding doc

## Tips

- Most teams nail Need and Target Group, fumble Stand Out, and over-stuff Key Features. The Stand Out section is where most strategy actually lives.
- If you can't articulate Stand Out in one sentence, you don't have differentiation — you have feature parity with extra steps.
- The canvas is a thinking tool. If filling it feels easy, you're not stress-testing enough.
- Vision Board sets the "why we exist." Strategy Canvas sets the "how we win this chapter." Both can be live at once.
