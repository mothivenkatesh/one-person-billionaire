# The Value Equation — Deep Dive

```
              Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value  =  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

To increase value, move ANY of the four levers. The lowest-scoring lever has the highest leverage.

## Lever 1: Dream Outcome (numerator)

The customer's actual emotional desire — not your product feature.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "AI email assistant" | "Close laptop at 5pm with inbox zero, every day" |
| "AI bookkeeping" | "Wake up Monday with last week's books done, audit-ready" |
| "AI cold outreach" | "Wake up to 3-5 booked sales calls every Monday" |
| "Lease analyzer" | "Reject the 2 toxic clauses on every lease before signing" |

How to extract the dream outcome:
- Ask "Why does that matter?" 5 times
- Find the *emotional* payoff under the functional benefit
- State it in the customer's voice, not yours
- Make it specific and time-bound

## Lever 2: Perceived Likelihood (numerator)

How confident the customer is that THEY will get the outcome.

Boost it with:
- **Testimonials** — named, with photos, with specifics ("Sarah saved 12 hrs/week")
- **Case studies** — before/after numbers
- **Demo videos** — show the outcome happening
- **Social proof** — "Used by 1,200 founders"
- **Risk reversal** — guarantees (see [guarantee-types.md](guarantee-types.md))
- **Authority signals** — "Built by ex-[trusted company]"
- **Logical proof** — show the math; show the mechanism

Note: claims without proof DECREASE perceived likelihood. "Saves you 50% time!" with no proof reads as marketing. "Anya Patel reduced her bookkeeping from 8 hrs/week to 1.2 hrs/week — see her case study" reads as truth.

## Lever 3: Time Delay (denominator)

How long until the customer gets the result. Less = more value.

Tactics to shrink it:
- Done-for-you setup (vs DIY)
- Instant first-result on signup ("see your first audit in 60 seconds")
- 24-hour onboarding SLA
- Trial-to-result < 1 day
- Quick-win staircase: small win on day 1, bigger on day 7, full result by day 30

If your time-to-first-result is > 14 days, you have a serious offer problem.

## Lever 4: Effort & Sacrifice (denominator)

What the customer has to do to get the outcome. Less = more value.

Tactics to shrink it:
- Done-for-you > done-with-you > do-it-yourself
- One-click integrations
- Pre-fill everything you can
- Default settings that work for 80% of cases
- White-glove onboarding for the first cohort
- Async approval gates (don't make them sit in a meeting)

Sacrifice includes opportunity cost, learning curve, identity change ("I'd have to admit I need help"), and team approval politics. Address ALL of these.

## How to score

For each lever, rate 1-10:

| Lever | Your offer | Best-in-class |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | __/10 | "Inbox zero by 9am, daily" = 10 |
| Perceived Likelihood | __/10 | "30 named testimonials + outcome guarantee" = 10 |
| Time Delay | __/10 | "First result in 60 sec; full outcome in 7 days" = 10 |
| Effort & Sacrifice | __/10 | "Done-for-you; one Gmail connection; 5 min total" = 10 |

The lever with the lowest score is your highest-ROI fix.

## Common misuses

- **Adding more features ≠ more value.** Often *reduces* perceived likelihood (overwhelming) and increases effort (more setup).
- **Lowering price ≠ more value.** Price is a positioning signal. Cheap = low value.
- **Faster ≠ better if it sacrifices proof.** "Instant AI report" with no audit trail is worse than "24-hour CPA-reviewed report."

## Quick wins

1. Add ONE named, dated testimonial → ↑ Likelihood by 1-2 points
2. Add an outcome guarantee → ↑ Likelihood by 2-3 points
3. Add done-for-you setup → ↓ Effort by 3-4 points
4. Add a 60-second first-result → ↓ Time by 2-3 points
5. Restate the dream outcome in customer's voice → ↑ Dream by 1-2 points

Each of these is a 1-day project. Stack 3-4 of them and your offer transforms.
