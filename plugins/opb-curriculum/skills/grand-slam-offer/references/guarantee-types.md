# The 4 Guarantee Types — Deep Dive

A guarantee is **risk reversal**. The customer's #1 unconscious objection: "what if I waste my money?" A specific guarantee answers it concretely.

## Type 1: Unconditional

> "100% money back. No questions asked. 30 days."

**When to use:**
- Low cost-to-serve (digital, near-zero marginal cost)
- High-trust market (your audience already knows you)
- High retention rate (you know few will refund)

**Pros:** maximum trust signal; lowest friction.
**Cons:** attracts tire-kickers and chronic refunders.

**Best for:** B2C info products, courses, low-touch SaaS.

## Type 2: Conditional

> "Money back if, within 30 days, you've completed onboarding (3 steps) and processed at least 50 sessions, AND you don't see a 50% time saving."

**When to use:**
- You want the customer to actually *use* the product before judging
- Your product needs ramp time to deliver value
- You want to filter out passive buyers

**Pros:** filters out tire-kickers; shifts effort to buyer; selects committed customers.
**Cons:** some real customers will feel the conditions are barriers.

**Best for:** B2B SaaS that requires setup; coaching programs; agent products with ramp.

This is the **default for AI agent products**. Use it.

## Type 3: Anti-Guarantee

> "All sales final. Here's why we're confident you'll succeed: [proof]."

**When to use:**
- Premium positioning (top 10% of market)
- You have *overwhelming* social proof
- Your customer profile is sophisticated (e.g., enterprise)
- Refunds would damage operations (custom/bespoke work)

**Pros:** signals confidence; selects only serious buyers; eliminates refund operations.
**Cons:** higher friction; only works at premium tiers with strong proof.

**Best for:** consulting, custom dev work, enterprise SaaS at $50K+ ACV.

**Risky for AI products**: typically don't have the proof yet. Avoid until you have 50+ testimonials.

## Type 4: Outcome / Implied Guarantee

> "If you don't [specific measurable outcome] within [timeframe], we [specific remedy: keep working free, refund + cash bonus, etc.]"

**When to use:**
- You can measure the outcome objectively
- You're confident in the outcome with average effort from customer
- Service or service-augmented product

**Pros:** maximum trust; aligns your incentive with customer outcome; premium pricing justified.
**Cons:** requires confidence + measurement infrastructure; risk of bad-fit customers exploiting it.

**Best for:** service businesses, AI products with measurable ROI ("3× pipeline or we work free until you do").

## The killer combo: Conditional + Outcome

For AI agent products in 2026, the highest-converting guarantee is:

> "Money back if you don't see [specific outcome] within 30 days, *provided* you've completed the 3-step setup and used the product at least N times. If you do see the outcome, we work free for an additional 30 days to entrench it."

This:
- Filters out non-committed buyers (Conditional element)
- Promises a specific result (Outcome element)
- Adds an upside bonus (the "free 30 more days" reward)

Close rate impact: typically 30-60% lift over a vague "satisfaction guarantee."

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| "We'll make it right" | Specify what "right" means and the trigger |
| Vague timeframe | Always state exact days |
| No conditions when you should have them | Add 1-3 reasonable conditions to filter buyers |
| Too many conditions | Keep to ≤ 3; more reads as weaseling |
| Hidden guarantee in T&Cs | Put it in the offer headline/CTA area |
| Guarantee that doesn't match the outcome | If your offer promises X, guarantee X — not "satisfaction" |
| Money-back only | Consider double-money-back, free continuation, or in-kind remedies |

## How to communicate the guarantee

Place it:
1. **Hero / above fold** — "Backed by our 30-Day [Specific Outcome] Guarantee"
2. **Pricing page** — full text of the guarantee, with conditions
3. **Onboarding email** — restate so the customer knows the clock is ticking on the trial
4. **Day 25 email** — "You're 5 days from your guarantee window — let me know if you're not seeing [outcome]"

A guarantee unstated is no guarantee. Lead with it.

## Math: should you offer one at all?

Calculate:
- Refund rate (if unknown, assume 5-15%)
- Cost to serve a refund (support time, payment fees, lost CAC)
- Upside conversion lift from offering it (typically 20-40%)

```
Net benefit = (Conversion lift × Order value) − (Refund rate × Order value × cost-to-serve multiplier)
```

For most AI products at $99-999/mo with 5-10% refund rate, the math says: **always offer a guarantee.** The conversion lift outweighs the refund cost by 5-10×.

The exceptions: anti-guarantee positioning at premium prices with overwhelming proof. Rare.
