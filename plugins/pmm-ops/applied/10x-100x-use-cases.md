## 10x use cases (efficiency multipliers)

**A. Personal POV flywheel.** Run `voice-engine` on your *own* archive (or borrow Logan's profile as a starter voice). Then: one insight → a 5-post week, each tied to a POV line, each passing the refusal check. This is the manual thing Logan does daily; automating it compounds distribution. *Ties to:* your `twitter-os`, `write-like-mothi`, the AI-role narrative ("show results built with AI").

**B. Whole-funnel voice consistency.** Distill the company/founder voice once; pipe it into every surface — homepage copy, nurture emails, sales one-pagers, even support macros. Logan's thesis: *brand + PMM are connected forces.* A single enforced Voice Profile makes that operational instead of aspirational.

**C. New-PMM / agency onboarding.** Hand a new hire (or a contractor) the Voice Profile of the best PMM in your category. They internalize the *taste* of the craft — what to reject, not just what to write — in a day. Logan's own hiring POV: functional excellence + fast learning beats industry priors; a Voice Profile is the fast-learning accelerant.

**D. Competitor narrative teardown.** Point `voice-engine` at a *competitor's* exec/PMM. Their distilled POV predicts their messaging moves and exposes the positioning whitespace they've vacated. (Logan: customers > competitors — so use this to find white space, not to obsess.)

**E. Messaging audit at scale.** Turn the Voice Profile's §5 Taste + the sweatshirt test into an automatic linter: paste any draft, get every hype/jargon/absolutist line flagged and rewritten boring-clear.

**F. Talk → content.** One recorded podcast/webinar transcript → 10 voice-consistent posts. Treat Q&A answers as voice samples; the profile keeps all 10 on-voice.

## 100x use cases (compounding assets / category positions)

**1. Voice OS (the product).** Productize the skill: a creator/founder uploads (or auto-harvests) their corpus → gets a *living* Voice Profile that updates as they post → a ghostwriting agent that drafts in their voice **and refuses off-brand takes** (the refusal layer is the moat; everyone else copies tone and misses taste). This is the strongest indie-product candidate to come out of this capability — and it's adjacent to assets you already have (`twitter-os`, `write-like-mothi`). The wedge: "AI that won't make you say things you'd never say."

**2. The AIPMM operator agent.** Full spec in `aipmm-agent-blueprint.md`. ~70% of the PMM job is already covered by MStack skills; voice-engine closed the voice/POV gap; the remaining gaps (always-on CI, launch-cadence, win/loss, content-engine) are a short build list. The differentiator vs "GPT with PMM prompts" is the **taste layer** — a real practitioner's POV the agent refuses to violate.

**3. Category POV-graph.** Harvest the top ~50 voices in a category (PMM, payments, devtools — whatever you're positioning into), distill each, and build a map of who-believes-what. The empty cells are *uncontested POV whitespace*. Then own one. This is literally the move that built Logan's following: he picked "messaging is the PMM's core / boring beats clever / specificity wins" and repeated it until it was his. A POV-graph industrializes finding that opening.

**4. Synthetic advisory board.** Distilled Voice Profiles of April Dunford, FletchPMM, Andy Raskin (all named in Logan's corpus) as *critique* agents: submit your positioning, get it reviewed the way each of them would. Strictly bounded to public corpus, always disclosed as a model — not impersonation. Rare and sticky; a paid layer on top of Voice OS.

**5. Brand-voice as enforceable infra.** For a company (Cashfree, or a client): every public artifact passes through one canonical Voice Profile before it ships — web, social, sales, support, release notes. Brand voice stops being a PDF nobody reads and becomes a gate in the publishing pipeline. Directly operationalizes Logan's "noticed (brand) + understood (PMM) at scale (growth)."

**6. Demand-gen flywheel.** `voice-engine` + `ci-monitor` (gap #2 in the blueprint) + a content-engine = an agent that watches the market and ships voice-consistent POV content on a cadence, compounding reach. The fully-assembled version of use case A, run for a brand instead of a person.

## How to pick (decision filter)

- **If the goal is your own leverage now** → A (personal POV flywheel). Lowest cost, compounds your visibility and the AI-results narrative.
- **If the goal is an indie product** → 1 (Voice OS). Clearest wedge, existing adjacent assets, definable moat (the refusal layer).
- **If the goal is the MStack PMM platform** → 2 (AIPMM agent). The blueprint already scoped the gaps.
- **If the goal is a positioning win for yourself or Cashfree** → 3 (POV-graph) → then own the whitespace it finds.

> Logan's own filter, applied to this list: *specificity wins.* Don't build all six. Pick one, get specific, back up the claim — then grow into the others.
