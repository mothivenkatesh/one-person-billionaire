# Info-Coaching / Consultant / Creator Funnel Patterns

Vertical-specific patterns to apply during **Phase 3 (Funnel Mapping)** and **Phase 4 (Synthesis)** when the target is a coach, consultant, info marketer, or creator-economy operator (i.e. selling information products, coaching, or done-for-you services to individuals).

These patterns surface in $200K–$3M/mo info-coaching funnels but appear less in SaaS, OSS, or B2B-enterprise targets. Use as a checklist of vertical-specific things to verify on top of the general 4-phase workflow in `SKILL.md`.

The 12 canonical templates in `funnel-templates.md` describe funnel *architecture*. This file describes the *operating patterns* that distinguish high-revenue from low-revenue operators within the same template.

---

## 1. The simplicity tell (counterintuitive: simpler = often more profitable)

The highest-revenue info-coaching funnels are visually unimpressive: a YouTube video, one landing page with a 10-minute VSL, one CTA ("Book a call" or "Buy"). Complexity is NOT correlated with revenue at the top end.

**What to check during Phase 3:**
- Count steps from CTA-click to call-booked. 1–3 = mature operator. 4+ = often sub-$100K/mo trying to compensate with funnel complexity.
- Hero copy softness: William Brown's *"Here's how I might be able to help you"* outperforms *"The exact 9-step system that prints money"* because the niche is burned out on confidence theater.
- Tripwires, order bumps, multi-step quizzes → operator is probably earlier-stage. Real scale operators trust their content to do the selling.

**Implication for Phase 4:** if the target's funnel is simpler than expected, that's a *positive* signal in this vertical, not "underbuilt." Don't recommend adding complexity in the 90-day plan.

---

## 2. Aggressive guarantees = burned-niche commodity tell

Pre-2024, money-back guarantees were a strong commitment trigger at the free→paid transition. Post-2024, in saturated info-coaching niches (business coaching, trading, fitness, agency-building), aggressive guarantees often signal a *commodity* offer compensating for weak proof.

**What to check:**
- Does the target lead with a guarantee or with proof? Lead-with-proof = mature. Lead-with-guarantee = often commoditized.
- Are testimonials specific (named client, recorded video, payment screenshot) or generic ("Sarah went from $0 to $10K!")? Generic = weak.
- Saturation test: if 5+ competitors in the niche use similar guarantee language, the guarantee no longer differentiates.

**Implication:** in the Phase 4 "What I wouldn't copy" section, flag aggressive guarantees in saturated niches as "category commodity tell" rather than "strong commitment trigger." Don't put them in the 90-day plan if the niche is mature.

## 3. Format innovation = category-of-one positioning

When a creator invents a new content format inside their niche, they get a "blue ocean" run. Examples observed in 2024–2026:
- **Hormozi:** Shark-Tank-style live business teardowns (case study disguised as entertainment)
- **Neuro Knowledge:** vboard format in manifestation niche
- **Sameer:** 1-vs-10 Q&A format (authority confronts 10 questioners)

**What to check:**
- What's the dominant format in the target's niche (talking-head, faceless tutorial, podcast clip)? Does the target use it or invent something distinctive?
- If novel, treat as a master-positioning move comparable in importance to messaging evolution. Add to Phase 4 as a strategic observation: *"Format innovation is the category-of-one play here."*
- Trace whether competitors have started copying the format — first-mover vs follower.

## 4. Instagram reels ≠ Instagram stories (analytical separation)

Most reverse-engineering lumps a creator's IG presence as one channel. In info-coaching they play radically different funnel roles:

- **Reels = Acquisition (top of funnel).** 30–60s loops, algorithmic distribution, cold audience. Content must serve Entertainment / Education / Inspiration. *Selling kills reach.*
- **Stories = Activation/Conversion (mid-late funnel).** 24-hour ephemeral, distribution to existing followers only, highest-trust audience on platform. *This* is where info-coaches sell.

**What to check during Phase 3:**
- Analyze reel content separately from story-highlight content in the AARRR map.
- Story highlights (saved permanently) reveal what the target wants newcomers to see — usually client results, BTS, FAQs. These are activation/retention assets.
- Look for a weekly story-sequence rhythm:
  - Mon: client-result + hard CTA
  - Tue: limiting-belief break / value
  - Wed: personal story / inspire
  - Thu: personal win (not just client wins)
  - Fri: soft CTA for free resource
  - Sat-Sun: lifestyle / Q&A
- Detection of this rhythm = high-conversion funnel, worth naming explicitly in Phase 4.

## 5. Free-course-as-primary-lead-magnet (the dominant 2025–2026 pattern)

In high-ticket info-coaching ($3K–$25K offers), the lead magnet has shifted from newsletter signups / PDFs to 3–10 hour "free course" videos on YouTube. Examples:
- **Caleb** (personal branding): 6-hour course → ~100K leads observed
- **FXG** (trading): 10-hour course → primary lead source
- Industry observation: free courses generate 10–100× the leads of newsletter opt-ins for this vertical

**What to check:**
- Does the target have a long-form (3+ hour) video on YouTube positioned as the core free resource?
- Is it cross-promoted from every other channel (IG bio, story CTAs, X pinned, website hero)?
- If YES → this is the dominant acquisition lever; map it as the primary entry point in Phase 3.
- If NO → the target is leaving the biggest lead-gen lever unpulled. Flag in Phase 4 as a strategic gap and put "build a 3+ hour free course" near the top of the 90-day plan.

## 6. YouTube watch-time = nurture proxy (the 7–14 hour rule)

Empirical pattern across info-coaching: buyers consume ~7–14 hours of a creator's content before purchasing. This drives the YouTube-as-printer dynamic.

**What to check:**
- Estimate the target's total YouTube minutes published. A creator with 50 hours of long-form content can nurture a buyer in one sitting; one with 50 reels cannot.
- The 7-hour rule means YouTube long-form > Instagram/TikTok at the nurture stage for high-ticket. If the target has high IG/X presence but low YouTube watch-time, their funnel is leaky at activation.
- Hormozi, Charlie Morgan, Iman Gadzhi, William Brown all post 1–4 YouTube videos per week regardless of channel size — the math is durable.

**Implication for Phase 4:** report YouTube watch-time (in hours) as a primary metric, not just subscriber count.

## 7. Messaging frame audit (what audience does the content attract?)

Different content frames attract different ICPs. Diagnose by skimming 5–10 of the target's most recent posts:

| Frame | Signal | Audience attracted | Conversion fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lifestyle / flex** | Lambo, penthouse, "what success looks like" | Broke / aspirational | Hard for high-ticket |
| **Value / how-to** | "How to get customers to stay forever" (Hormozi) | Business owners / operators | Best for high-ticket B2C |
| **Step-by-step beginner** | "The 7-step system" | Beginners craving direction | Mid-ticket sweet spot |
| **Personal story / relatability** | Rock-bottom stories, beliefs, worldview | Relaters who buy YOU | Best for personal-brand longevity |

**What to check:** which frame dominates? Strongest creators blend value + personal-story (Honia Carter is canonical — personal-branding content + her own beliefs/worldview).

**Implication:** add the messaging frame to Phase 4 as a strategic observation. A target attracting "broke aspirationals" with lifestyle content cannot easily move upmarket to high-ticket, and vice versa.

## 8. Pre-call sell-through assets (most analyses miss this layer)

Between booking-the-call and showing-up-to-the-call, top operators run a sell-through layer that converts as much as the call itself. Most reverse-engineering misses this because the assets are post-CTA.

**What to look for:**
- **Confirmation / thank-you page videos** — short (60s) videos handling top objections (cost, time, fit, "is this real?")
- **FAQ video stacks** — 60s answers to top 10 objections, embedded near booking CTA
- **Email value-drops** (NOT generic reminders) — pre-call emails that handle one objection each
- **Personalized outreach within 2 minutes of typeform submit** — setter calls every lead, qualifies, sends tailored pre-call assets

**How to access without booking:**
- Search YouTube for `"[target]" funnel breakdown` — competitors often expose these flows
- Look at affiliate marketers reviewing the target's program — they often screenshot the pre-call sequence
- Last resort: book the call yourself

**If inaccessible**, surface the gap explicitly in Phase 4: *"Pre-call sell-through layer not analyzed without booking. Conversion strength may be understated by 1.5–2× if this layer is well-built."* Don't silently skip.

## 9. Webinar + VSL combo = $200K+/mo scale signal

Pure call funnels (template #2 in `funnel-templates.md`) cap around $100–200K/mo. Pure webinar funnels (template #3) can scale to $1M–$5M/mo (Selby app: $5M in Jan 2026). Operators above $200K/mo who want to break the ceiling typically run BOTH:
- **Webinar** for warm leads who'll consume 90 minutes
- **VSL → call funnel** for those who won't

**What to check:**
- Recurring weekly webinar? Look for "Save your spot" landing pages with specific date/time.
- Separate VSL/call funnel for non-attendees?
- Both = $200K+/mo and using webinar as the scale lever.
- Only one = either earlier-stage or has chosen a smaller ceiling.

**Implication:** in Phase 4, the webinar+VSL combo is a scale-readiness signal — the target is either at $200K+/mo or actively trying to break that ceiling.

---

## 10. Production polish ≠ trust signal in 2026

Algorithm shift toward "interest media" (vs old "social media") means small, raw channels can outpace big polished ones. Don't penalize a target for low production if topic-fit and relatability are high.

**What to check:**
- Crisp thumbnails + tight editing OR raw (Loom-style screenshare, single-cut iPhone)? Either can win in 2026.
- Subscriber-to-view ratio: a 5K-sub channel getting 44K views per video is winning the algorithm despite low production.
- Don't conflate "looks unprofessional" with "isn't working" — the operator may be optimizing for relatability, which converts better than polish in this vertical.
