# CFO — D2C

## 1. Identity

The senior finance leader at a Series B+ D2C brand or roll-up. 12-25 years experience, often CA + MBA from IIM or US b-school, ex-Goldman / Big-4 / consulting / public company CFO. Reports to Founder/CEO and Board. Owns financial planning, treasury, working capital, audit, investor reporting, and capital raise/deployment. Lives in NetSuite / Tally / Zoho Books + Excel + Looker. Sees MDR + payment-related cost as a top-5 P&L line.

**Where you find them:** CFO Forum India, Indian CFO Summit, NextLeap CFO meetups, IPO-prep workshops, IBC/CII finance committees, MoneyControl webinars (panel speakers). Active on LinkedIn for finance-leadership content + IPO/capital-raise posts.

**Where they don't hang:** founder communities (peer-CFO networks instead), startup hackathons, dev events.

---

## 2. Top 3 pains (ranked by D2C-CFO interviews)

1. **Working capital tied in PG settlement cycle.** Settlement T+1 (or worse, T+2 for some categories) means ₹5-50 Cr is always in float. CFOs want to compress to T+0 to free working capital. **mothi wedge:** T+0 settlement on premium tier; faster on UPI/IMPS rails.

2. **MDR cost compounding.** As GMV grows from ₹100 Cr → ₹500 Cr, every 0.05% of MDR is real money — ₹2.5L/yr at ₹500 Cr scale. Negotiation is annual; they want volume-tier pricing + cross-product bundling math. **mothi wedge:** transparent volume-tier pricing + bundling discount when Payouts/International PG added.

3. **Capital + working-capital financing optionality.** Inventory + receivables tie up cash; debt is expensive. They want embedded finance (Capital / revenue-based financing) integrated into the payments stack. **mothi wedge:** Capital product offers receivables-backed working-capital advances priced 30-40% cheaper than traditional NBFCs.

**CFO secondary pains:**
- Reconciliation accuracy for audit (Big-4 audit-ready PG data)
- Multi-currency handling for international expansion (FX cost + hedging)
- Tax compliance on cross-border (TDS / GST / FEMA)
- Treasury management — auto-sweep into MF / overnight investments
- IPO-readiness: payments-data audit-trail completeness
- Vendor consolidation (CFO mandate: fewer vendor contracts)

- Audit observations (target: zero from Big-4)

## 4. Decision criteria when evaluating mothi

CFOs are deliberate but fast on quantified value. Decision criteria:

1. **MDR + working-capital math** (35%) — must be quantified
2. **Settlement speed (T+0 vs T+1) on working-capital impact** (20%)
3. **Capital product attach (receivables financing optionality)** (15%)
4. **Audit-trail + Big-4 audit-readiness** (10%)
5. **Vendor consolidation savings** (10%)
6. **Reference customers (peer CFOs)** (10%)

**mothi wins them when:**
- MDR + working-capital math delivered on first email (not "schedule a call")
- Capital product included in conversation (not just Payments)
- Peer-D2C-CFO reference: "{Peer-CFO} saved ₹X Cr on working capital + MDR"
- Audit-trail samples + Big-4 attestation shared
- Founder-to-CFO call (rare; signals seriousness)

**mothi loses them when:**
- MDR conversation is opaque ("call us for pricing")
- Settlement speed claim isn't backed by SLA contract language
- No CFO-level peer references
- mothi treats them as a "Payments-only" conversation (CFOs want Payments + Payouts + Capital integrated)

## 5. Language that resonates / turns them off

### Resonates

- **CFO math**: "T+0 vs T+1 settlement on ₹500 Cr/yr GMV = ~₹{X} Cr working-capital release; at 11% cost of capital, that's ~₹{Y} L/yr P&L"
- **MDR specifics**: "Volume-tier pricing: 1.85% up to ₹100 Cr, 1.65% beyond, 1.45% on cross-product bundling"
- **Capital ROI**: "Receivables-backed working capital at 14-16%; vs traditional NBFC at 21-26% — saves ~₹X Cr/yr on inventory financing"
- **Audit-readiness**: "Big-4 (Deloitte/PwC) attested PG data; sample audit-trail report below"
- **Peer-CFO precedent**: "{Peer-D2C CFO} cut payment-cost % of GMV from 2.1% to 1.7% in 12 months; happy to introduce"
- **IPO framing**: "Payments-data completeness for DRHP filing; we have a CFO-IPO-prep checklist"

### Turns them off

- Marketing speak
- Generic "schedule a demo"
- Hand-waving on MDR ("competitive pricing")
- Pitching only Payments without Capital + Payouts integration (CFO wants the full stack conversation)
- Long sales cycles (CFOs want decisive vendors)
- Lack of audit / compliance documentation
- Pitching to them as if they're operational (CFO is strategic + financial)

## 7. When this persona is the buyer / when not

### When THIS persona IS the buyer (or co-buyer)

- All Series B+ D2C deals — CFO sign-off required
- Capital / receivables-financing decisions
- Multi-product vendor consolidation
- IPO-prep payments-stack audit
- Treasury + cash management decisions
- **Pattern:** CFO + Founder co-decide; CFO drives MDR + Capital negotiation; Founder rubber-stamps strategic + brand fit

### When this persona is NOT the buyer (still influences)

- Operational-only product purchases (Payouts vendor swap) — Head of Ops drives, CFO signs off
- Marketing-tech purchases (CMO drives, CFO signs off only on contract value)
- **Pattern:** CFO is rarely the champion but is ALWAYS the gatekeeper for any deal >₹X
