# Head of Operations — D2C

## 1. Identity

The execution-focused leader at an Indian D2C brand who owns warehousing, fulfillment, vendor management, returns, and operational P&L. 8-15 years experience, often supply-chain or e-commerce-ops background (Amazon / Flipkart / Myntra alumni network). Reports to Founder/CEO or COO. Owns vendor payouts, COD-RTO, fulfillment SLAs, and ops cost. Lives in Excel + the ERP (Unicommerce/EasyEcom) + WhatsApp groups with logistics partners. Knows every percentage point of margin.

**Where you find them:** D2C operations forums (Inc42 events, YourStory D2C summits), supply-chain meetups, Shopify Plus partner events, Unicommerce/EasyEcom user groups, ex-Amazon/Flipkart WhatsApp networks. Active on LinkedIn for ops-hiring + benchmark posts.

**Where they don't hang:** founder communities (they're not founders), VC events, CRO/risk forums, dev communities.

---

## 2. Top 3 pains (ranked by D2C-research-corpus + Mothi's ops interviews)

1. **Vendor + influencer payout drag.** They run 50-300 vendor/influencer payouts monthly. Manual transfers via bank net-banking, reconciliation hell, late-payment trust damage, finance team spending 30-50 hours/month on payout ops. **Cashfree wedge:** Payouts API — T+0 single bulk file (IMPS/UPI/RTGS), auto-reconciled, white-label notifications.

2. **COD-RTO eating margin + tying up working capital.** ~25-35% of orders are COD; ~20-25% RTO rate; each RTO costs ₹150-300 + restocking + brand-trust hit. Returns inventory ties up working capital. **Cashfree wedge:** Pre-COD risk-scoring + Refund Velocity (instant refunds reduce return-cancellation friction).

3. **Reconciliation across PG + payout + shipping vendors.** End-of-day recon takes 2-4 hours; mismatches require investigation. Multiple vendor stacks = multiple recon files. **Cashfree wedge:** Unified Payments + Payouts + reconciliation dashboard cuts recon time 60-70%.

**Ops-head secondary pains:**
- Refund processing speed (customer expectation: instant; reality: 5-7 days)
- Cash-flow visibility across PG settlements + COD remittances
- Shipping partner reconciliation (Delhivery/Bluedart commission disputes)
- Fraud chargeback management
- Marketplace fee reconciliation (Amazon/Flipkart fee splits)

## 4. Decision criteria when evaluating Cashfree

Heads of Ops are pragmatic. Decision criteria:

1. **Operational cost reduction (in hours + ₹)** (35%) — measurable in their P&L
2. **Payout reliability (T+0 + delivery rate >99%)** (20%)
3. **Reconciliation simplification** (15%)
4. **Refund speed + customer-facing impact** (15%)
5. **Integration time + ERP compatibility** (10%)
6. **Pricing** (5%) — last; ops accepts premium for time-savings

**Cashfree wins them when:**
- Payouts ROI calculation: "current 40 ops-hours/month → 8 ops-hours/month"
- Live demo of bulk-payout file + reconciliation dashboard
- Unicommerce / EasyEcom integration shown working
- Refund Velocity shown vs incumbent's 5-day refund cycle
- Peer-D2C-ops-head reference: "We saved ₹X/mo in finance-team hours"

**Cashfree loses them when:**
- Pricing-first conversation
- Cannot demo integration with their specific ERP
- No D2C ops references on the call
- Migration risk story is weak

## 5. Language that resonates / turns them off

### Resonates

- **Ops-cost math**: "40 ops-hours/month at ₹500/hr = ₹2.4L/yr; Cashfree Payouts cuts that to 8 hours = ₹1.9L savings"
- **Specific metrics**: "T+0 settlement; bulk-file processing of 5000 payouts in <5 min; 99.7% IMPS delivery"
- **ERP integration**: "Native Unicommerce + EasyEcom + Shopify Plus connectors; sample webhook flow"
- **Peer-D2C ops stories**: "Plum cut payout-recon hours from 38h/mo to 6h/mo using Payouts API"
- **Returns/refund framing**: "Instant refunds reduce 'where's my money' tickets by 60%; CSAT lift measurable"
- **Working capital**: "T+0 vs T+1 settlement = ₹X working-capital improvement at your monthly GMV"

### Turns them off

- Strategic / founder-level framing (they're operational; talk numbers)
- Marketing speak ("transform your ops")
- Long emails (>120 words)
- Generic "schedule a demo" without ops-cost math
- Webinar invites
- Pitching to them as if they own product strategy (founder/CEO does)

## 7. When this persona is the buyer / when not

### When THIS persona IS the buyer

- Payouts API decisions (operational vendor, ops-head decides)
- Refund-processing infrastructure
- Reconciliation tooling
- Pre-COD / RTO risk-scoring tools
- ERP-integration vendor decisions
- **Pattern:** ops-head decides + signs (with founder rubber-stamp on contracts >₹X)

### When this persona is NOT the buyer (still influences)

- Payments core decisions (founder + CFO own)
- Strategic vendor consolidation (founder + CFO own)
- International PG (founder + CFO own)
- Capital / Pay Later (CFO + founder own)
- **Pattern:** when ops-head is buyer, close fast; when not, build them as champion to influence founder/CFO
