# Skill 22: Affiliate Program vs Ambassador Program — Two Distinct Models

**Category:** Marketing & Growth | **Phase:** 4. Scale

> **Key Principle:** Affiliates are broad and transactional (anyone can join, earn from referrals). Ambassadors are selective and relational (invite-only, volunteer, about leadership). Both have a place but serve different purposes.

## What It Is

Two fundamentally different approaches to leveraging your user base for growth. Ben described how Notion piloted an affiliate program alongside its established ambassador program, and the distinction matters for anyone designing their community strategy.

## The Core Distinction

### Ambassador Program
- **Who:** Highly selective. Application-based. Long waitlist.
- **Size:** Small (onboard about 20 every other month at Notion)
- **Compensation:** None. Volunteer only.
- **Motivation:** Passion, access, community, status, early access to features
- **Activities:** Run community groups, host events, create content, provide feedback, mentor new users
- **Relationship:** Deep, personal, long-term (1-year commitment)
- **Goal:** Community leadership, user education, brand evangelism
- **Feel:** Exclusive club of community leaders

### Affiliate Program
- **Who:** Broad. Anyone with an audience can join.
- **Size:** Large (potentially thousands)
- **Compensation:** Financial. Earn money from referral signups.
- **Motivation:** Revenue from their existing audience
- **Activities:** Share referral links, promote the product in their content
- **Relationship:** Transactional, low-touch
- **Goal:** Top-of-funnel signups, reach new audiences
- **Feel:** Open marketplace for growth partners

## When to Use Each

### Start with Ambassadors (Early Stage)
- When you need genuine evangelists, not just link sharers
- When quality of engagement matters more than volume
- When you can't afford to pay for referrals
- When you need feedback and product input from power users

### Add Affiliates Later (Growth Stage)
- When you have a proven product that converts well
- When you want to tap into audiences you can't reach through ambassadors alone
- When you have the infrastructure to track referrals and pay commissions
- When ambassadors already exist and affiliates are the next growth layer

## Step-by-Step SOP: Launching an Affiliate Program Alongside Ambassadors

### Step 1 — Ensure Ambassador Program Is Healthy First
- Don't launch affiliates before ambassadors are established
- Ambassadors provide the authentic community foundation
- Affiliates without ambassadors creates a mercenary dynamic

### Step 2 — Design the Affiliate Offer
- Decide on commission structure (percentage, flat fee, tiered)
- Create unique tracking links for each affiliate
- Build a simple dashboard where affiliates can see referrals and earnings
- Make it easy to join (low barrier, unlike ambassador application)

### Step 3 — Let Ambassadors Be Affiliates Too
- Anyone in the ambassador program should be eligible for the affiliate program
- But the affiliate program should NOT automatically grant ambassador access
- This rewards your most loyal community members

### Step 4 — Maintain the Distinction
- Keep the ambassador program selective, volunteer, and relationship-based
- Keep the affiliate program open, transactional, and results-based
- Don't blur the lines: ambassadors are leaders, affiliates are promoters

### Step 5 — Measure Differently
- Ambassadors: activity level, contributions, community health, qualitative impact
- Affiliates: signups, conversions, revenue attributed, cost per acquisition

## Common Pitfall

Many companies skip the ambassador program and go straight to affiliates. This creates a community built on financial incentive rather than genuine love. When the commissions stop or a competitor offers better rates, the affiliates leave. Ambassadors who are there because they genuinely love the product don't leave when incentives change. Build love first, then layer on financial incentives.
