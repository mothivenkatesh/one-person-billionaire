# Skill 26: Investment Thesis — Using Community Signals to Evaluate Startups

**Category:** Investing | **Phase:** Framework

> **Key Principle:** Look for organic community love as an investment signal. Facebook group excitement, users building things without being asked, and genuine emotional connection with a product are leading indicators of breakout potential.

## What It Is

A framework for using community signals (organic user enthusiasm, grassroots adoption, user-generated content) as leading indicators when evaluating early-stage startup investments.

## How Ben Uses Community Signals

### The Land Investment Example
One of Ben's early investments was Land, a fractional real estate platform. Part of what attracted him was seeing genuine excitement in their Facebook group. This grassroots enthusiasm was a signal that metrics alone wouldn't capture.

### The Notion Pattern as a Template
Ben evaluates companies through the lens of what he saw at Notion:
- Are users talking about this product without being paid or asked to?
- Are users creating content, templates, or resources around it?
- Are users organizing meetups or communities on their own?
- Is there emotional connection, not just utility?
- Do people love it enough to evangelize it to friends?

## The Signal Framework

### Strongest Signals (Highest Conviction)
- Users creating businesses or careers around the product
- Organic community groups forming without company involvement
- Users creating educational content (YouTube, blog posts, courses)
- Passionate word-of-mouth

### Strong Signals
- Active, engaged Facebook groups or Reddit communities
- Users sharing screenshots and setups on social media
- High NPS or enthusiastic customer testimonials
- Users defending the product in public discussions

### Moderate Signals
- Good engagement metrics (DAU/MAU, retention)
- Positive reviews on app stores or review sites
- Growing waitlist or signup rate

### Weak Signals (Lower Conviction)
- Paid influencer campaigns generating buzz (manufactured, not organic)
- High social media followers without engagement
- Press coverage without user enthusiasm

## Step-by-Step SOP: Evaluating Community Signal

### Step 1 — Search for Organic Mentions
- Search Twitter, Reddit, YouTube, TikTok for the product name
- Look for unprompted, genuine posts from real users
- Absence of organic mentions is a yellow flag, not a dealbreaker

### Step 2 — Check for Community Formation
- Does a Facebook group, Discord server, or subreddit exist?
- Who created it? (Company-created vs user-created matters enormously)
- How active is it? What's the tone?

### Step 3 — Look for Creator Activity
- Are users making YouTube videos, blog posts, or tutorials?
- Are these paid/sponsored or genuinely organic?
- Is anyone building templates, plugins, or tools on top of the product?

### Step 4 — Assess Founder Understanding of Community
- Does the founder understand their community's motivations?
- Do they have a plan for community-led growth?
- Are they listening to and engaging with early power users?

### Step 5 — Project the Flywheel Potential
- Can this scale into a Notion-like flywheel?
- Does the product have the characteristics for community formation? (Customizable, visual, deep, multi-use)
- Is there a path from organic enthusiasm to sustainable creator economy?

## The Anti-Pattern

Companies with high marketing spend but no organic community love are a warning sign. If the only way users hear about you is through ads, the growth is fragile. Ben looks for the opposite: products where users become evangelists without being asked.
