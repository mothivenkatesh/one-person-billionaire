# Skill 15: Scrappy Team Scaling for Community Operations

**Category:** Operations | **Phase:** 4. Scale

> **Key Principle:** Run a massive community operation with minimal headcount. Use contractors, leverage your community members, and spin out programs to other teams as they mature.

## What It Is

A practical approach to scaling community operations when you have limited headcount and budget. The key is strategic use of contractors, empowering community members to do the heavy lifting, and graduating mature programs into other parts of the organization.

## Ben's Team at Notion

Even at scale, Notion's community team was remarkably lean:
- Ben (Head of Community)
- 1 community manager (focused on ambassador program)
- 4-5 contractors at approximately 50% capacity each
- Hiring 1 additional community manager

This small team supported an operation that included hundreds of ambassadors, thousands of influencer partnerships, 100+ community groups across 50+ languages, a global event program, a certification program, a template gallery, and a champions community.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Phase 1 — Solo Operator (1 person)
- You do everything: outreach, events, content, ambassador management
- Focus ruthlessly on the 1-2 programs showing the most traction
- Don't try to do everything. Find where the energy is and pour into that.
- This phase is about proving value and learning what works

### Phase 2 — Add Contractors (1 person + 2-4 contractors)
- Hire part-time contractors for specific programs or tasks
- Even a couple hours per day or week from the right person makes a huge difference
- Key hiring principle: find people who are PASSIONATE about your product, not just skilled
- Contractors can handle: data entry and tracking, outreach, event logistics, content moderation

### Phase 3 — First Full-Time Hire (2 people + contractors)
- Your highest-leverage hire: a community manager to own the ambassador program
- The ambassador program is the engine that powers everything else
- This hire frees you to focus on strategy, new programs, and cross-functional partnerships

### Phase 4 — Spin Out Mature Programs
- As programs grow, they deserve dedicated ownership
- Notion's influencer program graduated to having its own full-time dedicated hire
- Events got their own person who eventually ran Block by Block
- These people may sit in different teams (marketing, partnerships, customer success)
- Programs taking on wings and growing into their own functions is a sign of success

## Outsourcing Strategies
- Outsource data entry and tracking (monitoring 100+ community groups monthly)
- Outsource initial creator outreach and relationship management
- Outsource event logistics while keeping event strategy in-house
- Outsource template gallery curation and moderation

## The Community as Your Team

The biggest scaling lever is not hiring more people. It's empowering your community members to do the work:
- Ambassadors run ALL community groups (not your team)
- Ambassadors organize events (you just fund them)
- Power users create templates (you just curate the gallery)
- Certified consultants handle enterprise onboarding (you just run the certification)

## Hiring Advice from Ben

When hiring for community roles, especially early on:
- Find someone who is very passionate about what you're doing
- Ideally they're already a user of your product
- Look for early signs of being a super fan
- You need energy, creativity, and perseverance
- Passion for the product trumps community management experience
