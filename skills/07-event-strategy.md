# Skill 7: Event Strategy — Community-Organized Beats Company-Organized

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 3. Formalize

> **Key Principle:** Fund and empower community members to organize events. Don't try to run everything yourself. Community-organized events are more powerful, more scalable, and more authentic.

## What It Is

A strategy where you fund and support community members to host their own events rather than organizing everything centrally. You only own one or two flagship events per year. Everything else is community-driven.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Notion saw people organizing informal meetups and decided to provide funding. This scaled to events happening almost every day in different cities. They tested running their own events too, but pivoted away because community-organized events were far more powerful.

The one exception: Block by Block, a bi-annual flagship conference. The first one was during COVID on Hopin, with tens of thousands of signups.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Phase 1 — Observe Natural Gatherings
- Watch for anyone organizing informal meetups around your product
- Even 2-3 people meeting up is a signal worth paying attention to

### Phase 2 — Fund, Don't Organize
- Offer funding/sponsorship to people who want to host events
- Provide: budget for venue/food, swag, brand assets
- Do NOT provide: rigid format, mandatory agenda, corporate oversight

### Phase 3 — Scale Through Ambassadors
- Your ambassador program is the source of event organizers
- Each ambassador in a city = potential event host
- Provide a playbook but let them adapt to local culture

### Phase 4 — Own Only the Flagship
- Run 1-2 big annual events yourself
- Everything else: community-organized
- Your conference is the gathering point; local events sustain momentum

### Phase 5 — Adapt to Circumstances
- When in-person isn't possible, pivot to digital immediately
- Don't wait. The community needs to gather regardless of format.

## Why Community-Organized Wins
- **Scalability:** One team can't organize events in 50+ cities. Ambassadors can.
- **Authenticity:** Local hosts know their audience better than HQ.
- **Cost efficiency:** Small sponsorships go far.
- **Ownership:** Organizers are deeply invested in making it great.
- **Diversity:** Events happen in languages and formats you'd never think of.
