# Skill 8: Certification Program for Power Users

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 5. Systematize

> **Key Principle:** When you see users already consulting and onboarding companies to your product, formalize it with an assessment and directory. You're not creating behavior — you're legitimizing existing behavior.

## What It Is

A formal assessment program that turns your most skilled users into certified consultants. Creates a professional services ecosystem, a public directory for enterprise adoption, and a reason for power users to go deeper.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Ben noticed users monetizing their expertise — helping companies onboard to Notion. Rather than starting from scratch, Notion saw this happening organically and built infrastructure to legitimize and scale it: an assessment, a scoring exercise, and a public consultant directory.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Spot the Behavior First
- Look for users already helping companies adopt your product
- Check if freelancers offer consulting services around your product
- If nobody is doing this yet, it's too early for certification

### Step 2 — Design the Assessment
- Written assessment testing deep product knowledge
- Practical exercise (build something, solve a real scenario)
- Clear scoring rubric with pass/fail threshold

### Step 3 — Build the Public Directory
- Public page listing all certified consultants
- Searchable by specialty, location, language
- Link to each consultant's website/profile

### Step 4 — Drive Demand
- When enterprise customers ask "Who can help us implement this?" → point to directory
- Add directory links to onboarding flows and help docs
- Creates economic incentive for more people to get certified

### Step 5 — Let the Ecosystem Compound
- Certified consultants hire their own teams
- They create courses, templates, content to market their services
- They become your most invested ambassadors (livelihood depends on your product)

## Real Results
- Dozens of certified consultants earning entire livings from Notion consulting
- Multiple consultants earning over $1M/year with hired teams
- A professional services ecosystem that drives enterprise adoption without Notion needing its own services team
