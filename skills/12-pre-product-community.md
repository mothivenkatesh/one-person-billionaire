# Skill 12: Pre-Product Community Building (The IT Kit Example)

**Category:** Marketing & Growth | **Phase:** 1. Observe

> **Key Principle:** Before your product exists, build community around your target audience's shared interests — not your product. Talk to them, find what excites them, build something small around THAT.

## What It Is

A strategy for building an audience around your target customers BEFORE your product is ready. The anchor isn't your product. It's a shared interest that unites your target audience.

## How Ben Did It: The IT Kit Story

Ben worked with a pre-product company targeting IT leaders. No product meant nothing for people to connect around. So he talked to IT leaders 1:1 and discovered they loved discovering new tools (similar to Product Hunt).

He hired a contractor, built "IT Kit" — a Product Hunt clone for IT people. Curated the initial list from the IT leaders he'd spoken to, added an upvote function. It went viral in IT communities and captured thousands of email addresses.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Talk to Your Target Users (1:1)
- Schedule 15-20 video calls/coffees with target audience
- Ask: What excites you? Where do you spend time online? What do you wish existed?
- Document everything. Look for patterns.

### Step 2 — Find the Shared Interest
- Analyze conversations for recurring themes
- The interest does NOT need to relate to your future product
- It must be something they genuinely care about enough to engage with

### Step 3 — Build a Small, Useful Thing
- Keep it simple. A curated directory, newsletter, voting site, resource list.
- It should be buildable in days/weeks, not months
- Must provide immediate, standalone value

### Step 4 — Launch, Capture, Nurture
- Share in communities where your audience hangs out
- Include email capture
- When your product is ready, you have a warm audience that already trusts you

## Key Insight from Ben

The community anchor doesn't have to be your product. It has to be something your target users genuinely care about. By providing value around THEIR interest, you earn trust before you ever need to sell anything.
