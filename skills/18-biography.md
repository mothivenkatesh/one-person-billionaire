# Skill 18: Full Biography & Origin Story

**Category:** Career & Personal | **Phase:** Framework

> **Key Principle:** Every builder is made through compounding experiments. eBay flipping, dog walking, hackathons, side projects, military intelligence, community obsession — each chapter fed the next.

## Background

Builder, community leader, angel investor. Formerly Head of Community at Notion (5 years, employee ~15 to 600+). Now running a $6M micro angel fund and building Next Play. Lives in Tel Aviv with his wife. Grew up in the US (Bay Area and New York), moved to Israel for high school, served in IDF intelligence.

## The Full Arc

### Chapter 1: The eBay Hustler (High School)
Ben's grandfather gave him camera equipment to sell. He got hooked on the process. Started posting ads on Craigslist and putting up flyers offering to sell people's stuff for a cut. He was running this operation out of his bedroom: photographing items, writing listings, packing boxes, shipping from his house.

### Chapter 2: The Hackathon Organizer (Military Era)
While serving in IDF intelligence, Ben spent his off-duty time hacking on side projects. He organized Innovation Israel, one of Israel's first hackathons (~2011). The concept was for people to work on projects that showed Israel in a different light.

### Chapter 3: International Humus Day
During the hackathon, Ben discovered Nutella Day and thought hummus deserved the same treatment. He bought hummusday.com, built a simple website, picked May 13th as the date (completely arbitrary), and forgot about it. A month later it went viral. Now it's a globally recognized food holiday.

### Chapter 4: Product Hunt & Community Obsession
Ben became deeply embedded in the Product Hunt community, hosting events and discovering new products. This is where his instinct for community building sharpened.

### Chapter 5: Falling in Love with Notion
Ben discovered Notion on Product Hunt. It took 4-5 tries for it to click. He tweeted his setup, people loved it. He built notionpages.com (20-30K visits/month). He started a Facebook group. He bought allthingsnotion.com and notionforbusiness.com.

### Chapter 6: Getting Into Notion (No Application)
Ben cold-emailed Notion's founder asking about the API. Said he'd be in SF next week. He was lying — he booked a trip. Visited the office. Met Camille Rickets on her second day. The next day he was working on Notion's template gallery. No interview. No job application. No job description. He created the role by proving it needed to exist.

### Chapter 7: Building Notion's Community (5 Years)
Joined when Notion was about 15 people. Built and scaled: ambassador program, template gallery, influencer program, community groups in 50+ languages, certification program, champions community, Block by Block conference, and more. Left when the company was around 600 people.

### Chapter 8: Angel Investing & Next Play (Now)
Started angel investing by apprenticing with another investor for several years. Raised a $6M micro fund. Made approximately 50-60 investments. Building Next Play (nextplay.so) — curated gatherings for people exploring career transitions, now happening in NYC, London, Paris, Berlin, Austin, Boston, Bangalore.

## The Thread That Connects Everything

Every chapter shares the same DNA:
- **Curiosity** drives the initial discovery
- **Building** something small and shipping it quickly
- **Generosity** (helping others before extracting value)
- **Taking shots** (booking flights, sending cold emails, launching projects)
- **Letting serendipity decide** the outcome

## Ben's Core Philosophy

- Be curious. Spend time seeing what people are building.
- Have fun. When something interests you, go deep without overthinking.
- Take shots. Cold emails, side projects, reaching out. Most won't work. A few will change your life.
- You can make an impact pretty easily. Sometimes it takes 2 hours.
- Build the relationship before asking for anything. Deliver value first.
