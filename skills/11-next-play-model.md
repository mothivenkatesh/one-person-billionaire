# Skill 11: The Next Play Model — Community for People in Transition

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 2. Seed

> **Key Principle:** People exploring what's next feel lost and isolated. Build curated, intimate gatherings that connect them with others in the same boat. Start with dinners, scale through hosts.

## What It Is

A community model for professionals in career transitions. Built around curated in-person gatherings, a newsletter, and a host network.

## How Ben Built It

Started hosting small dinners (10-15 people) for people in transition, inspired by Erik Torenberg's On Deck model. Evolved into Next Play (nextplay.so) with hosts in NYC, London, Paris, Berlin, Austin, Boston, Bangalore. Added a biweekly newsletter and podcast.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Start with Dinners
- Curated, intimate gatherings of 10-15 people
- Invite-only to maintain quality and intimacy
- Format should encourage honest conversation about uncertainty

### Step 2 — Understand the Shared Need
- People in transition want: connection, inspiration, opportunities, accountability
- Build around that emotional need, not a product

### Step 3 — Scale Through a Host Network
- Find people in other cities who want to host
- Provide: brand, database access, format, playbook
- They adapt to local context

### Step 4 — Add Content Layers
- Newsletter every 2 weeks with curated opportunities
- Podcast with interviews about how people figured out their next move
- Shared database of people and opportunities

### Step 5 — Keep It Pure
- Don't monetize too early
- The network's value compounds over time
- Many outcomes will be serendipitous connections you didn't plan

## Connection to Angel Investing
Next Play creates a natural dealflow and value-add network:
- Founders in transition → investment opportunities
- Operators in transition → recruiting pipeline for portfolio companies
- The database becomes a talent and dealflow engine
- Only works because the community provides genuine standalone value
