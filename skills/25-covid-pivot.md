# Skill 25: The COVID Pivot Playbook — In-Person to Digital Community

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 4. Scale

> **Key Principle:** When in-person isn't possible, pivot immediately. Don't wait for perfect conditions. The community needs to gather regardless of format. Digital events can be equally powerful if done right.

## What It Is

A playbook for rapidly transitioning community programs from in-person to digital when circumstances change. Based on Notion's experience pivoting during COVID, when in-person events were a huge part of their community strategy.

## The Context at Notion

Before COVID, in-person events were a really big thing for Notion's community. Meetups were happening almost every day in different cities. Community members were organizing gatherings, workshops, and hackathons. Then everything shut down.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Acknowledge and Adapt Immediately
- Don't wait to see if the situation improves
- Don't put community on pause
- The community needs connection MORE during disruption, not less
- Announce the pivot quickly and transparently

### Step 2 — Translate In-Person Formats to Digital
- Meetups become video call hangouts or webinars
- Workshops become live interactive sessions on Zoom
- Conferences become virtual events (Notion used Hopin for Block by Block)
- Casual networking becomes structured virtual coffees or small group calls
- Not everything translates 1:1. Some formats need to be reinvented for digital.

### Step 3 — Equip Your Community Hosts
- Ambassadors who were hosting in-person events need new tools and guidance
- Provide recommendations for video platforms, formats, and best practices
- Share what's working from other hosts in other cities
- Recognize that some hosts will adapt naturally and others will struggle

### Step 4 — Launch Your Digital Flagship
- Use the moment to launch something ambitious digitally
- Notion launched Block by Block as an entirely online event
- Tens of thousands of people signed up
- Sometimes constraints create opportunities you wouldn't have explored otherwise

### Step 5 — Rethink What Scale Means
- Digital events remove geographic barriers
- A local meetup that got 20 people in person might get 200 online from around the world
- International participation becomes trivially easy
- Time zones become the new constraint (not venue capacity)

### Step 6 — Decide What Stays Digital Post-Crisis
- Some things work better digitally (global conferences, international AMAs)
- Some things need to return to in-person (local community bonding, networking)
- Use the forced experiment to learn which formats belong where
- Many communities find a hybrid model works best long-term

## Broader Lesson

The COVID pivot is really about resilience and adaptability. Community programs that depend on a single format (only in-person, only one platform) are fragile. The best community strategies work across multiple formats and can adapt when circumstances change.
