# Skill 9: Influencer Marketing on a Startup Budget

**Category:** Marketing & Growth | **Phase:** 2. Seed

> **Key Principle:** You don't need a large budget. Start with small/medium creators. Organic, authentic videos perform best even when sponsored. This is the most underleveraged channel for startups.

## What It Is

A practical system for running effective creator partnerships without a large marketing budget. Focus on authenticity over polish, small creators over mega-influencers, and patience over speed.

## Budget Reality
- Start with whatever you have. Even a few hundred dollars per video.
- Small/medium creators are more affordable AND have more engaged audiences
- Package deals: 2-3 short TikToks for one fee
- You do NOT need mega-influencers

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Define Your Niche Segments
- Where does your product naturally fit? (For Notion: productivity + education)
- What topics does your product solve for? That's where your creators are.

### Step 2 — Build Your Outreach List
- Search YouTube, TikTok, Instagram for creators in your niche
- Prioritize those who mention your product or use competing products
- Sweet spot: 5K-100K followers (affordable and engaged)

### Step 3 — Outreach
- Short, genuine messages. Reference their specific work.
- Propose collaboration or offer free account (3-Month Nurture for cold contacts)

### Step 4 — Content Approach
- Let creators be themselves. Do NOT over-script.
- Real demonstrations of actual use > polished ads
- Provide key talking points but let them present in their own style

### Step 5 — Tracking
- Unique UTM link for every creator partnership
- Track signups and conversions per creator
- Measure cost per signup by creator tier and platform
- Double down on what works

### Step 6 — The Flywheel
- Creators watch other creators
- More videos → more creators discover your product → inbound replaces outbound
- The first few months are hardest. Then momentum compounds.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Spending entire budget on one big influencer instead of 10 small ones
- Over-scripting content that comes across as fake
- Pitching partnership on first message to someone who's never used your product
- Not tracking UTM links (flying blind on ROI)
- Giving up after month one before the flywheel kicks in
