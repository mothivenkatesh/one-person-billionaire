# Skill 27: Personal Brand Through Compounding Side Projects

**Category:** Career & Personal | **Phase:** Framework

> **Key Principle:** Each side project compounds into the next. eBay taught marketing. Hackathons taught community building. Notion pages got him into Notion. The pattern is: build, ship, let serendipity connect the dots.

## What It Is

The meta-skill underneath everything Ben does: continuously shipping small projects, each of which builds skills, reputation, and connections that feed into the next opportunity. No single project needs to be a grand slam. The compounding effect over time is what creates an outsized career.

## Ben's Compounding Chain

1. **eBay Flipping (High School)** — Learned marketing, customer acquisition, logistics
2. **Dog Walking** — Learned service business basics; realized eBay was more scalable
3. **Innovation Israel Hackathon (~2011)** — Learned event organizing, community building
4. **International Humus Day** — Learned viral mechanics, how little effort can create outsized impact
5. **Product Hunt Community** — Learned how tech communities work, event hosting
6. **notionpages.com + Notion Facebook Group** — Learned building around a product you love; led to meeting Notion team
7. **Notion Community Leadership (5 years)** — Learned everything: ambassador programs, influencer marketing, decentralized community, events, certification, measurement, team building
8. **Corona Crush** — Learned social platforms, matchmaking communities, viral growth
9. **Notion Wedding/Home Templates** — Learned going viral with personal content, leaning into attention
10. **Angel Investing** — Learned deal evaluation, founder relationships, portfolio construction; led to $6M fund
11. **Next Play** — Building a host network, scaling gatherings internationally, newsletter/podcast

## The Pattern

Every project follows the same loop:
1. **Curiosity** — Something catches Ben's attention
2. **Build fast** — He creates something in hours or days, not months
3. **Ship publicly** — He puts it out there without worrying about perfection
4. **Let it breathe** — He doesn't over-manage outcomes
5. **Harvest connections** — The project creates relationships, skills, or opportunities
6. **Feed the next project** — What he learned informs what he builds next

## Step-by-Step SOP: Building Your Own Compounding Chain

### Step 1 — Start Shipping Small Things Now
- Don't wait for the perfect idea. Build something this week.
- A simple website, a community group, a curated list, a small event
- The bar for your first project is: can I build this in a weekend?

### Step 2 — Make Everything Public
- Tweet about what you're building
- Post on Product Hunt, Reddit, relevant communities
- Share screenshots, learnings, and progress openly
- Each public project builds your visible track record

### Step 3 — Don't Worry About Failures
- Most projects won't go viral. Most won't turn into careers.
- Ben has projects that went nowhere. That's expected.
- The cost of a failed side project is near zero. The upside of one that works is life-changing.

### Step 4 — Follow Your Curiosity, Not a Plan
- Ben didn't plan a career path from eBay to Notion to angel investing
- He followed what interested him and said yes to opportunities
- The connecting thread is curiosity and willingness to build

### Step 5 — Take Shots
- Book the flight you can't afford to meet the team
- Send the cold email to the founder you admire
- Launch the holiday for a food you love
- Most shots miss. A few change everything.

## The Compounding Math

If you ship one small project per month for 3 years, that's 36 projects. If even 3 of them create meaningful opportunities (a job, a relationship, a community, a business), your career trajectory changes completely. Ben's chain of about 11 major projects over 15 years produced: a career at Notion, a $6M fund, a global community, media coverage, and a personal brand that opens doors. None of it was planned. All of it compounded.
