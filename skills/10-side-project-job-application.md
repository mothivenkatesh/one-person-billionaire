# Skill 10: The Side Project as Job Application

**Category:** Career & Personal | **Phase:** Framework

> **Key Principle:** Build something valuable around a company's product before they hire you. Do the job before you have the job. Let results speak louder than resumes.

## What It Is

A career strategy where you demonstrate your value to a company by building something useful around their product BEFORE applying for a job.

## How Ben Did It

1. Discovered Notion on Product Hunt. Took 4-5 tries to click.
2. Tweeted his Notion setup — people loved it
3. Built notionpages.com (template sharing site) → 20-30K visits/month
4. Bought additional domains: allthingsnotion.com, notionforbusiness.com
5. Started a Facebook group for Notion users
6. Actively helped people onboard to Notion for free
7. Cold-emailed Notion's founder asking about the API
8. Said he'd be in SF next week. He was lying. Booked a trip.
9. Visited the office. Met Camille Rickets on her 2nd day.
10. Next day was working on Notion's template gallery with her
11. Evolved into a full-time role. No formal interview. No job application.

There was no job description for "Head of Community" at Notion. Ben created the role by demonstrating its value before it existed.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Find a Product You Genuinely Love
- This only works if your passion is real. You can't fake months of effort.

### Step 2 — Build Something Useful Around It
- A resource site, gallery, community, newsletter, tutorial series, tool
- It must fill a gap the company hasn't filled yet

### Step 3 — Make It Public and Let It Grow
- Share on Product Hunt, Twitter, Reddit, relevant communities
- Track metrics: visitors, signups, engagement

### Step 4 — The Company Will Notice (or Reach Out)
- If they don't find you, reach out with what you've built
- You're not saying "I think I could be useful." You're saying "Here's what I already built."

### Step 5 — Make Yourself Available
- Be willing to travel, adapt, and start quickly
- Ben booked a flight to SF on the spot

## Ben's Broader Career Advice
- Be curious: spend time seeing what people are building
- Have fun: when something interests you, go deep without overthinking
- Take shots: cold emails, side projects, reaching out. Most won't work. A few will change your life.
