# Skill 24: Using Your Own Product as Community Infrastructure (Dogfooding)

**Category:** Operations | **Phase:** 2. Seed

> **Key Principle:** Build your community page, ambassador hub, and resources using your own product. This showcases capabilities, simplifies updates, and turns your community into a live demo.

## What It Is

Using your own product as the platform for your community operations, resources, and public-facing community pages. This creates a live demonstration of your product's capabilities while making community management easier.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

### 1. External: The Public Community Page
The notion.community page was built entirely in Notion. Every visitor was simultaneously learning about the community AND experiencing the product.

### 2. Internal: Community Operations
The community team used Notion internally to manage operations: tracking ambassadors, monitoring community groups, planning events, managing the influencer pipeline, and coordinating with contractors.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Build Your Community Portal in Your Product
- If your product supports public pages or websites, use it for your community landing page
- This turns your community page into a product demo
- Every visitor sees what your product can do while learning about the community
- Updates are easy: your team can edit without engineering

### Step 2 — Use Your Product for Ambassador Management
- Create a shared space where ambassadors can access resources
- Track ambassador activity and contributions in your product's database features
- Share meeting notes, event guides, and brand assets through your product
- Ambassadors using your product deepens their expertise

### Step 3 — Run Internal Community Ops in Your Product
- Pipeline tracking for influencer outreach
- Ambassador applications and selection
- Event planning and logistics
- Community group tracking dashboard
- Contractor task management

### Step 4 — Make It Visible
- Share screenshots of how your team uses the product internally
- Let ambassadors see the internal pages (where appropriate)
- Blog about or present your internal setup at events
- This creates inspiring content AND showcases product depth

## Why This Works

- **Credibility:** Your team using the product for complex operations validates capabilities
- **Discovery:** Every community page visit is a product experience
- **Efficiency:** No need to build separate community tools
- **Content:** Your internal setup becomes content for templates and tutorials
- **Feedback:** Using your own product for real work surfaces bugs and improvements

## When This Doesn't Work

- If your product isn't suited for public pages or content management
- If your product is too specialized (e.g., a developer tool that can't host a landing page)
- In these cases, use your product internally for ops but build the public portal elsewhere
