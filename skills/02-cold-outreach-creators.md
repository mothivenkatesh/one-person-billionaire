# Skill 2: Cold Outreach to Creators (The 3-Month Nurture)

**Category:** Marketing & Growth | **Phase:** 2. Seed

> **Key Principle:** Don't pitch a partnership on day 1. Give them the product free, set a 3-month reminder, let them fall in love organically, then partner.

## What It Is

A system for reaching out to influencers and creators who don't yet know or use your product, converting them into genuine fans through patience, then partnering with them for authentic content.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Notion's influencer program started by reaching out to YouTubers who mentioned Notion organically. But to scale, they needed creators who didn't know Notion yet. Ben's breakthrough was not pitching immediately. Instead, he would give creators free premium access and set a 3-month follow-up. By then, many had fallen in love with the product organically, making the eventual partnership authentic.

## Step-by-Step SOP: Creator Outreach Pipeline

### Phase 1 — Identify Targets (Week 1)
- List creators in your product's niche (e.g., productivity, education, design)
- Prioritize those who have mentioned your product OR use adjacent/competing products
- Check follower counts — start with small to medium creators (more receptive, more affordable, often more engaged audiences)
- Build a spreadsheet: name, platform, follower count, niche, contact info, any mention of your product

### Phase 2 — First Touch (Week 1-2)
Send a short, genuine outreach message:
- Introduce yourself by name and role
- Reference something specific they've made that you liked
- Ask if they're familiar with your product
- **If YES:** Propose a collaboration (sponsored video/post)
- **If NO:** Offer a free premium account. Say "No pressure, just thought you might enjoy it. Happy to chat in a few months about how it's going."

### Phase 3 — Nurture Period (Month 1-3)
- Set a calendar reminder for exactly 3 months out
- Do NOT pester them during this period
- Let them discover the product naturally through actual use
- Optional: engage lightly with their content on social (like, comment) to stay visible

### Phase 4 — Re-engage (Month 3)
- Follow up casually: "Hey, how's it going with [product]?"
- Many will respond with genuine enthusiasm
- Now pitch the partnership: "Would you be interested in creating a video about how you use [product]? We'd love to sponsor it."
- They propose a price, you negotiate
- Sign a simple contract
- They produce the content
- You provide a UTM link for tracking signups

### Phase 5 — Flywheel Activation
- Creators watch other creators. As more videos go live, more creators discover your product organically
- Over time, inbound partnership requests start replacing outbound cold outreach
- The first few months are the hardest. After that, momentum builds exponentially

## Key Insight from Ben

The organic, real videos where creators genuinely used and loved Notion performed dramatically better than scripted or forced content. The 3-month nurture was the secret: it ensured authenticity because the creator actually had time to form a real opinion.

## Budget Reality
- Start with a very small budget (small and medium creators are affordable)
- Package deals work well: 2-3 short TikToks for one fee
- You do NOT need mega-influencers to see results
- Ben called this the most underleveraged channel for startups
