# Skill 19: Champions Community — Enterprise Customer-to-Customer Program

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 5. Systematize

> **Key Principle:** Build a dedicated space where your enterprise/team customers learn from each other, share use cases, and get inspired. It becomes a scalable alternative to 1:1 customer success.

## What It Is

A curated community specifically for customers on enterprise or team plans. Unlike the ambassador program (for external evangelists), the champions community is for internal champions — the people inside companies who advocate for and drive adoption of your product within their organizations.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Notion built the Champions Community as one of only two centralized communities they ran (the other being the ambassador program). It was designed for people who were champions of Notion on team and enterprise plans.

The community became a scalable way for customers to learn from each other, get inspired by what other companies were doing, and discover new use cases.

## Why This Is Different from the Ambassador Program

| Aspect | Ambassador Program | Champions Community |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Who | External evangelists, creators | Internal advocates at customer companies |
| Goal | Spread product externally, grow community | Deepen product adoption within orgs |
| Value to member | Platform, access, visibility | Learning, use cases, peer connections |
| Value to company | Top-of-funnel growth, content, events | Retention, expansion, enterprise adoption |
| Selection | Application-based, selective | Customer-based (must be on team/enterprise plan) |

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Identify Your Champions
- Look for power users within your customer accounts
- These are the people pushing for adoption, training their teams, building workflows
- Your customer success team can identify them, or use product usage data
- Start with 20-30 of your most engaged enterprise customers

### Step 2 — Create a Dedicated Space
- Separate from your external community (different audience, different needs)
- Use a platform that feels premium and curated (private Slack, dedicated community platform)
- Keep it invite-only to maintain quality and make members feel special

### Step 3 — Design for Peer Learning
- The primary value is customers learning from OTHER customers
- Facilitate use case sharing: how is Company A using the product vs Company B?
- Host regular sessions where champions present their setups and workflows
- Create a library of customer use cases organized by industry, team size, and function

### Step 4 — Connect Champions to Your Team
- Give champions direct access to product managers and engineers
- Host product roadmap previews and feedback sessions
- Champions who feel connected to the product team become the strongest internal advocates

### Step 5 — Scale the Value
- The community becomes a self-sustaining peer learning network
- New customers immediately get access to hundreds of proven use cases
- Champions help each other solve problems, reducing support burden
- Customer stories from the community become case studies for your sales team

## Connection to Business Outcomes

- **Retention:** Customers connected to peers are less likely to churn
- **Expansion:** Seeing use cases from other companies inspires broader adoption
- **Advocacy:** Champions who feel connected become vocal advocates externally too
- **Feedback:** A curated group of enterprise users provides the highest quality product feedback
