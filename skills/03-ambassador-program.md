# Skill 3: Building an Ambassador Program from Zero

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 3. Formalize

> **Key Principle:** Start with 10-20 genuine super fans. Give them access, not cash. Make them feel special and connected to your team. Let them inspire each other.

## What It Is

A structured volunteer program that turns your most passionate users into organized community leaders. Ambassadors get exclusive access and support in exchange for evangelizing, creating content, running communities, and hosting events.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Notion's ambassador program started with about 10-20 people Ben had identified as super fans (someone in Japan organizing meetups, someone in Korea creating content, etc.). He spent his early time having video calls with each of them, understanding their motivations. This grew into a formal application-based program that onboarded about 20 people every other month, with a long waiting list.

Ambassadors were entirely volunteers, never paid. The program ran on one-year commitments, and ambassadors could leave anytime. Despite being unpaid, many stayed for years because the access, community, and platform they gained was more valuable than cash.

## Step-by-Step SOP

### Step 1 — Build the Seed Group (Month 1)
- Start with 10-20 people you've identified through your Super Fan Scanning
- Invite them to a private Slack or Discord channel
- Spend time on 1:1 video calls with each person
- Ask every one of them: "Why are you doing this? What could we do to support you to do more?"
- Document their motivations, ideas, and current projects

### Step 2 — Design the Value Stack (No Cash)
What ambassadors receive:
- Early access to features before public release
- Direct communication line to the team (dedicated Slack channel)
- AMAs with founders and leadership
- Events to meet each other and the team
- Swag (design it well — make people proud to wear it)
- Visibility: featured on your community page
- Certification or badge
- Their feedback actually gets heard and acted on

### Step 3 — Open Applications (Month 2-3)
- Post an application form on social media
- Keep it selective: onboard approximately 20 people every other month
- Create a waitlist (scarcity drives perceived value)
- Selection criteria: demonstrated leadership, existing community contributions, genuine passion for the product
- Make the application itself signal what you're looking for

### Step 4 — Structure the Program
- One-year volunteer commitment (can leave anytime, no hard feelings)
- Clear code of conduct published publicly
- Dedicated community manager to run the program (your highest-leverage community hire)
- Regular touchpoints: monthly AMAs, quarterly virtual meetups, annual gathering

### Step 5 — Empower, Don't Control
- Let ambassadors run their own communities on whatever platform they choose
- Let them organize events in their own way and language
- Let them create content in their own style
- Your job is to provide resources, recognition, and access — not to dictate activities
- Ben's team was not even admin of the community groups ambassadors created

### Step 6 — Track and Nurture
- Monthly check: what percentage of ambassadors are active?
- What types of contributions are they creating? (events, content, templates, community moderation)
- Which regions or languages are growing? Which need help?
- Recognize top contributors publicly
- Send appreciation (Ben's team sent personalized thank-you videos)

## Key Insight from Ben

The ambassadors became the powerhouses behind everything. They started almost all the community groups, hosted events, created templates, and built YouTube channels. Many built entire businesses around Notion, with some earning over one million dollars per year from courses, consulting, and template sales. The program worked because it was never top-down — it empowered people who were already doing the work.

## Results at Notion
- Ambassadors started and ran ALL community groups (Notion moderated none)
- r/Notion grew to 200K+ subscribers (community-run)
- Notion Vietnam reached 220K members (started by 2 ambassadors)
- Notion Arabic reached 40K members
- Multiple ambassadors now run full businesses earning six and seven figures
