# Skill 1: Spotting Super Fans Before They Know They're Super Fans

**Category:** Community Building | **Phase:** 1. Observe

> **Key Principle:** Monitor organic behavior across platforms. The best community members are already doing the work before you find them.

## What It Is

The ability to identify early passionate users who have the potential to become evangelists, before they've self-identified as such. Ben spotted people organizing meetups, writing books, starting YouTube channels about Notion — all without being asked.

## How Ben Did It at Notion

Ben spent a lot of early time at Notion searching Twitter, YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit for anyone mentioning Notion organically. He also went into Intercom and product analytics to find users with the highest activity, then cross-referenced them with social media presence to see who had followers and influence.

He would look for specific behaviors: organizing a meetup around the product, writing a blog post or book about it, starting a YouTube channel, creating templates or resources for other users, answering questions in forums, or building side projects on top of the product.

## Step-by-Step SOP: Weekly Super Fan Scan

### Step 1: Social Listening (30 min/week)
- Search Twitter/X for your product name, filter by recent
- Search YouTube for your product name, filter by upload date last week
- Search Reddit, TikTok for new mentions
- Check Product Hunt for any related launches or discussions

### Step 2: Internal Data Cross-Reference (20 min/week)
- Pull top 20 most active users from your product analytics (Intercom, Mixpanel, etc.)
- Cross-reference with social media profiles
- Look for users with existing audiences (even small ones)

### Step 3: Signal Scoring
Rank each person on these signals:
- Already creating content about your product (highest signal)
- Organizing or attending events related to your space
- Active in related communities/forums
- Has an existing audience (YouTube, Twitter, blog)
- Using your product in creative or unexpected ways

### Step 4: Personal Outreach
- Send personal DM or email to top 3 new finds each week
- Not a template blast. Genuine, specific message referencing what they're doing
- Ask questions, not make requests: "I noticed you built X — that's amazing. What made you want to do that?"

### Step 5: Track Everything
- Log all outreach in a tracker: name, platform, behavior spotted, date contacted, response
- Review monthly to identify patterns in where your super fans are clustering

## Key Insight from Ben

At Notion, every major community program started because Ben spotted organic behavior first. Templates existed before the gallery. Meetups existed before they funded them. YouTubers existed before the influencer program. The pattern is always: observe first, then systematize.

## When to Use This
- You have a product in the market with active users
- You're trying to understand if community-led growth is viable
- You want to identify your first 10-20 potential ambassadors
- You're planning an ambassador or advocacy program
