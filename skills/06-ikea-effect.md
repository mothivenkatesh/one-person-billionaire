# Skill 6: The IKEA Effect — Product-Community Loop

**Category:** Marketing & Growth | **Phase:** Framework

> **Key Principle:** Products that require user effort create stronger emotional attachment, which drives sharing, teaching, and community formation. The learning curve is a feature, not a bug.

## What It Is

A framework for understanding why certain products naturally generate vibrant communities while others don't. Products that give users a blank canvas and require effort to build something create powerful emotional attachment that drives community formation.

## The Loop

1. **Blank Canvas** → User encounters a flexible, customizable product with a learning curve
2. **Effort Investment** → User spends time building something personalized
3. **Pride & Attachment** (The IKEA Effect) → User feels ownership and emotional connection
4. **Sharing Impulse** → Pride drives sharing (screenshots, templates, tutorials)
5. **Teaching Drive** → Learning curve creates opportunity to teach others
6. **Connection Desire** → User wants to connect with other builders
7. **Community Formation** → Sharing + teaching + connecting = organic community
8. **Flywheel** → Community creates more content → more people discover product → cycle accelerates

## Products Where This Works Best
- **Customizable:** Users can make it their own (Notion, Figma, Lego)
- **Visual:** What users build is shareable and impressive
- **Collaborative:** Work can be shared, duplicated, remixed
- **Deep:** There's always more to learn and master
- **Multi-use:** Can be used for many different purposes

## Why Excel Doesn't Have This

Ben was asked why Notion has a vibrant community but Excel doesn't. His answer: Excel lacks the brand, the story, the aesthetic, and the emotional connection. The playfulness and aesthetics of the product matter enormously. People either don't get Notion or they love it — there's no in between. Having fans who feel something is what drives everything.

## How to Apply This
- Don't over-simplify your product to the point where users invest zero effort
- Design for the moment of pride when someone completes something
- Make it easy to share what users build (publish, duplicate, export)
- Celebrate what users create (feature them, retweet them, showcase them)
- Build the template gallery and community infrastructure to capture this energy
