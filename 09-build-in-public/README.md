# Lesson 09: Build in Public as Distribution

## TL;DR

Build in public is the single highest-leverage distribution channel for solo agent operators in 2026. It's not optional — every successful indie hacker since 2020 has done some version of it. But most engineers do it wrong: posting features instead of stories, polished updates instead of struggle, vanity metrics instead of revenue. This lesson covers what works.

## Core idea

```
Build in public = sharing the work, the numbers, and the lessons
                  in real time, on a public timeline,
                  so the audience compounds with the product.
```

Why it works:
- People follow **journeys**, not products
- Strangers root for visible underdogs
- Every post is an SEO/AEO snippet that compounds
- Other builders amplify other builders
- It pre-builds your launch list

Why most engineers fail at it:
- They post **features**, not feelings
- They go silent for 6 weeks, post once, then go silent again
- They pretend everything's working
- They optimize for engagement instead of right-customer attention

## How it works in practice

### The 4 archetypes that work in 2026

**1. The Numbers-Public Founder**
Posts MRR, churn, ARR, runway, weekly. Pieter Levels, Tony Dinh, Marc Lou. Works because numbers are universally legible and rare to see honestly.

**2. The Build Diary**
Posts the messy daily process: bug, fix, design choice, customer convo. Daniel Vassallo, Adam Wathan (early Tailwind). Works because the *process* is the content; readers feel like insiders.

**3. The Teach-While-You-Build**
Every problem you solve becomes a how-to post. Hamel Husain, swyx, Eugene Yan. Works because teaching attracts both customers (who learn from you) and peers (who amplify you).

**4. The Hot-Take Operator**
Strong opinions on what works, doesn't, why. Jason Lemkin (B2B SaaS), Sahil Bloom (one-person businesses). Works because opinions are searchable and quotable. Risky because hot takes age badly.

**Pick one.** Mixing all four reads as inauthentic. Most successful operators are 80% one archetype, 20% another.

### Platform priority for 2026

| Platform | Why | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **X (Twitter)** | Highest signal for tech/builder audiences. Discovery is alive again post-Grok. | Daily |
| **LinkedIn** | Higher conversion to enterprise buyers. B2B SaaS founders should be here. | 3–5×/week |
| **YouTube (long-form)** | Compounding SEO. Slowest start. Best ROI at month 18+. | Weekly |
| **Substack / blog** | Owned audience. Email-based = highest conversion. | Weekly–biweekly |
| **Reddit / niche forums** | Direct customer access. Most underrated. | As-needed (no spam) |
| **TikTok / Shorts** | Consumer products only. Hard for B2B. | Skip unless C |
| **Threads / Bluesky** | Not yet at critical mass for B2B. Skip. | Skip |

**Default starter pack for an agent-product solo operator: X + Substack + occasional LinkedIn.** Don't try to do all platforms. Compound on one.

### The post anatomy that works

Bad post:
> *"Excited to share that we shipped a new feature today!"*

Good post (numbers archetype):
> *"$847 MRR this week. Cancellation: 1 customer (paid 4 months, switched to a competitor that has X integration). Adding X to the roadmap. Lesson: integrations are not features, they're entry/exit doors."*

Good post (build diary):
> *"Spent 6 hours debugging why the agent kept calling search_web instead of fetch_url. Turned out my tool description for fetch_url started with 'Search...' instead of 'Fetch...' Naming matters more than the function."*

Good post (teach):
> *"3 things I learned shipping our first agent to paying customers: (1) Per-token billing kills you. (2) The first 10 customers won't churn for the right reasons. (3) Skill loading > stuffed prompts. Thread on each ↓"*

Pattern: **specific number / specific story / specific lesson.** Vague posts go nowhere. Specific posts compound.

### What NOT to share

- Customer names without permission
- Investor conversations
- Co-founder disputes
- Mental health spirals (post-recovery is OK)
- Hot takes about ex-employers / ex-customers (always backfires)
- Anything you'd be embarrassed to read in 5 years

### The 6-month commitment

Build-in-public has a **dead period** of months 1–4 where nothing seems to work. ~50 followers, ~5 likes per post, 0 customers from social. This is normal. The compounding starts at month 5–6 when:

- One post hits 100K views unexpectedly
- A specific cohort starts following (your future customer base)
- Other builders start sharing your stuff
- You start getting DMs from prospects

If you quit at month 3, you got nothing. If you push through to month 6+, you have a permanent distribution channel.

## Common traps

| Trap | Why |
|---|---|
| Posting feature announcements | Nobody cares. Post stories. |
| Hiding the numbers | Numbers are why people follow you. Show them. |
| Begging for engagement ("RT if you...") | Reads desperate, kills the brand |
| Posting only when you have something to say | Algorithm punishes silence; compound requires consistency |
| Optimizing for follower count instead of right-customer count | 1,000 right followers > 100,000 wrong ones |
| Outsourcing to a "ghostwriter" | The voice is the value. Authentic > polished. |
| Stopping at month 3 | The compounding starts at month 6+ |
| Treating it as marketing | It's relationship-building. Marketing is downstream. |

## The honest math

Followers alone don't pay. But followers → email list → customers does:

```
1,000 followers      →  100 email subscribers  →  3-5 paying customers
10,000 followers     →  1,000 email subscribers →  30-50 paying customers
```

Conversion rate: ~0.3-0.5% follower → paying customer per year. So 1,000 right followers = ~$3-5K in your first year. **The compounding shows up in year 2+.**

For agent products at $99-999/mo, this is significant ARR.

## Exercise

**30-day daily commitment.**

- Pick one archetype
- Pick one platform
- Post once per day for 30 consecutive days
- Each post: specific number / specific story / specific lesson
- Track: views, replies, follows, **and DMs from prospective customers**

End of 30 days, evaluate:
- Are the right people finding you? (check follower industry mix)
- Are you getting any inbound? (DMs > likes)
- Are you enjoying it? (sustainability matters)

If yes to all three: continue for 11 more months. If no: change archetype or platform, run another 30-day test.
